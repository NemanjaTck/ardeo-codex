As the shadows of the Zenari armies grew over the Luvani desert, the vigilant Kadir Vanguard and Zargon scouts swiftly relayed the news to Nohenjo Daro: the enemy had crossed the K’uhad River. In response, the Kadir Vanguard was summoned from all corners of the desert, bolstering the city’s defenses in preparation for the imminent siege.

Zenari forces, known for their might, were well-equipped. In addition to their robust ground units, they had two powerful Hel cannons, forged in the fiery depths of Vuldrush. Zaeor's arsenal also included two fleets of Zenari Roc riders and a pair of battle-equipped airships—a symbol of power and advancement in war technology.

The siege commenced with an intense bombardment, interrupted only by sporadic skirmishes on the ground between the elite Kadir riders and the stalwart Earth Zenari defenders. General Menezaar Ardor, tasked with the city’s defense, played his strategic hand with precision, baiting the youthful Zaeor into impatience - an error the seasoned commander was ready to exploit.

On the fifth day, as tensions reached their peak, Zaeor, driven by rage, commanded his forces to mount a full-scale assault. The ground troops advanced with rams and ladders to scale the walls, while archers and mages unleashed a relentless barrage of fireballs and arrows. Above them, Roc riders and airships soared, casting imposing shadows over the battlefield. However, Menezaar had anticipated this move. He signaled his most adept mages to ascend the towers and employ arcana to direct the ballistas. The bolts, now infused with guided arcane energy, were an unforeseen blow to Zaeor's forces.

The real turning point came when the Zenari ladders reached the walls. Suddenly, a massive explosion light up the sky. As Zaeor and his troops looked upwards in disbelief, the remnants of a destroyed airship hurling towards them like a meteor. Simultaneously, Roc riders began to drop from the sky, picked off one by one by the precise magical assaults from the city’s defenders. The Zenari mages, outmatched by the superior sorcery of Nohenjo Daro’s, faltered. Amidst the chaos, Zaeor, witnessing the devastation of his troops, called for the retreat.

General Menezaar emerged from this conflict a hero, celebrated and respected. To this day, the anniversary of this major victory is commemorated throughout the Zargon as Victory Day - a memory of resilience and strategic brilliance.
