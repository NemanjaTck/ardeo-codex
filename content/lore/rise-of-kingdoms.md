#### Zargon

Growth and prosperity of Nohenjo Daro led to the rise of Zargon society. Desolate expanse of the Luvani desert expanded around the magical aura of the Lumis river, making the once-barren soil fertile. Temples and grand libraries began to rise across the city, magnificent in their design, their spires reaching clouds. First buildings across the city were built resembling elven architecture. As the people of Nohenjo Daro grew more distant from their elven roots, the architecture began to change, relying on sand stones and white colors as a symbol of purity.  

Legends whispered through Luvani desert and beyond suggested that the city’s roots may lay deeper than the foundations of stone and sand. Rumors hinted at the city’s rapid growth and expansion being connected to a natural source of Arcana, hidden beneath the very streets of the city. 

As the Zargon expanded their dominion, they started sending scouts to Luvani. Oases became waystations, and caravansaries dotted the region, becoming small settlements. The desert, however, held a secret close to its hearth. Vicious sand storms guarded the uncharted depths of Luvani, claiming the lives of many who dared to cross inside.
One extraordinary tale was found in one of the larger Zenari cities. Gaish Danak, a young mage of the Rock Zenari, dared to unravel the ever existing enigma of Luvani. In the shadow of night, he separated from his comrades, venturing alone in the storms. His last message, etched into the pages of a travel diary of his party, sent by him, telepathically, in the middle of the night: “I have breached the veil. Something is off, though. Time falters, air is different. Do my eyes deceive me, or those are floating islands on the horizon?” Despite valiant efforts to retrieve him, Gaish Danak vanished. 

With challenging conditions of the desert, the nomadic settlements of Zargon remained modest, with only a handful of capable traders, warriors and travellers. Nohenjo Daro, as the first human bastion, grew into the biggest city on the Westglade. Soon, they adopted meritocracy, entrusting leadership to the wisest among them. The Kadir Vanguard unit, named in tribute to the desert goddess, patrolled the city and were entrusted to guard the caravans crossing the trade routes set between the caravansaries. Zargon, never having a standing army, wielded a different kind of power. Their mages and priests, feared across the continent, cast shadows that stretched beyond the shifting dunes, marking the presence of a society defined by arcane mastery. 


#### Tsardom of Moravia

In the year of 1012 BG, lands of Moravia rested under the watchful eyes of the Circle of Elders. Ethereal marks of their elven ancestors, the slick build and grace, elven ears - all were fading memories. Veles and Volod were the two big bastions, governed by a Knyaz and his esteemed family. In smaller cities and villages, the presence of the Obor-Knyaz affirmed a semblance of hierarchy and order. Both Knyaz and Obor-Knyaz still pledged their allegiance to the wise Circle of Elders.

At the time, Knyaz Bogoslav Meronevich of Veles, had a vision of unity. A single banner under which people of Moravia would gather together, leaving behind past alliances and loyalties and creating a powerful empire. Knyaz Oleg Vitarin of Volod, lacking Bogoslav's insight and drive, agreed to support the grand vision. Through adept diplomacy and the silent, yet potent support of military prowess, Bogoslav achieved a seamless transition of power without spilling a single drop of blood. He proclaimed himself Tsar Bogoslav Meronevich of the grand Empire of Moravia, a realm split into the entities of Knyazhevina Veles and Knyazhevina Volod. His son, Ivan, inherited the title of Knyaz of Veles while Oleg Vitarin, result of his unquestionable support for Bogoslav, remained the esteemed leader of Volod. The Circle of Elders remained as the trustful consultants in the new empire.

Nohenjo Daro is always going to remain the first Human city, yet the creation of the Empire of Moravia marks the awakening of the Human race. This pivotal moment was the start of a long rule of Meronevich, one of the most powerful dynasties in the history of Ardeo.

#### Kingdom of Ragdar

In the year of 1153 BG, a powerful energy flowed through Ulfheim, filling the land with life. Now a magnificent city, it was standing tall, as a guardian of the Eden river. Every stone, every tree witnessed Ulfheim’s rapid expansion.

Yet, with expansion came chaos, creeping through the streets and alleyways of Ulfheim like a silent shadow. Without a sovereign to unify its growing population, without the law to guide its moral compass, Ulfheim was a city governed by wealth and power. From the chaos of anarchy, arose the self-proclaimed aristocracy. Rich families wielded their wealth as a symbol of authority. In the absence of a throne, gold became the crown, giving them unchallenged decree of power.
Soon, they commissioned their own guards, loyal not to the city, but to the coin. The city was surrounded by a vast amount of natural resources, some of which were scarce in the other parts of the continent. Result of the voracious appetite of the ruling caste was the growing number of trade routes, threading their paths through the forests and deserts, across plains and mountains, connecting Ulfheim with distant lands of Ardeo. 

Sweat and blood of the working caste was the unacknowledged part of the narrative of Ulfheim’s prosperity. Their voices remained unheard, their toils unseen.
In the coming years, scouts were returning with reports of enigmatic metals, found in the harsh Bargahai mountains in the north. The elves, their artistry with silver mining spanning through half a millennium, were acquainted with the possibilities and value of this noble metal. Yet, the treasures born from the silent depths of Bargahai were completely unknown, a material darker and harder than silver itself. The leading nobles of Ulfheim, their eyes glowing of greed, were lured towards the north. As the scouts confirmed findings of silver along with this new material, first expeditions, an ensemble of miners and warriors, travelled to Bargahai. In the icy winds and silent snows, the world’s first known iron mines were born. To shelter the miners from the chill of winter and all unseen threats that lurked in the harsh cold nights, a walled settlement, small and robust, was built adjacent to the mines. With time, the settlement grew. It was christened Helburg, a name that would, in the centuries to come, resound as a fort city - a sentinel of the distant cold north.

In an age of rising crime and anarchy on the streets of Ulfheim,  the young noble Gundohar of the esteemed House Lamga rose to seize power. Losing his father in youth, Gundohar was forged into a master swordsman under his uncle's tutelage. With ambition burning like wildfire, he rallied his loyal guards and orchestrated a merciless purge of the noble families, killing every single family head until there was none left, claiming the throne as Ulfheim’s first monarch. 

His reign was marked by tyranny and cruelty. Gundohar, alongside his generals, instituted a grim system of slavery. The noble houses, restored under his rule, wielded absolute control over the common folk, crushing any semblance of freedom. Whispered tales spoke of Gundohar's dark dealings with distant cities like Yuzunu and Segmut, trafficking slaves from the Eastern continent's far shores. Though Gnomes and Halflings still dwell in Ulfheim and Helburg, none could trace their lineage to Gundohar's deeds.

Known amongst his subjects as Gundohar the Cruel, his rule was unchallenged for years. Yet, the scars of his betrayal – the beheading of family elders – festered in the hearts of many. In the villages around Ulfheim and Helburg, organized cells of resistance emerged, their initial aim: to dismantle the brutal child labor system and provide the escape routes for the young to safer lands north and west across the ocean. Their efforts, though valiant, were swiftly crushed by Gundohar's special regiments, known as the Cloaks for their bloodstained red cloaks – a symbol of their brutal magecraft, learned from ancient elven tomes.

Few withstood the Cloaks' initial onslaught. As years of Gundohar's oppression passed, a flicker of hope emerged. The resistance, rekindled, brought forth two promising leaders: Eormen, a skilled warrior of the southern forests, and Illric, a sorcerer cloaked in the darkness of Helburg. They had no surnames, a cruel edict from Gundohar to erase the heritage of the common folk, leaving rights to family names to noble houses only. Illric first struck, seizing a Helburg mine in a bold uprising. This act of defiance ignited Eormen's campaign in the forests, leading to key strategic victories. Yet, the walls of Ulfheim remained impenetrable, and their ragtag forces were crushed by Gundohar's disciplined army.

In this desperate hour, a twist of fate emerged. Esteban, a young wizard of the Cloaks, sick from the horrors he witnessed, sought out Eormen's camp under the cloak of night. Revealing the secret passages beneath Ulfheim, he guided them to Gundohar's quarters. There, in the silence of night, they assassinated the tyrant king. Eormen, fueled by a lifetime of pain and loss, unleashed a vengeful fury upon the Lamga household, sparing none. Standing before the innocent eyes of Gundohar's young, he hesitated, but it was too late. The Lamga lineage was extinguished in a night drenched in blood. 

The death of Gundohar plunged his followers into chaos. Eormen swiftly consolidated power in Ulfheim, while Illric, with Esteban's aid, wrestled control of Helburg. However, a tension grew between the two leaders. Eormen, haunted by his own brutality, enacted a law banning magic throughout the kingdom. Illric, branded an outlaw for his sorcery, led his followers to the frostbitten Bargahai mountains.

Thus, the Ragdar Kingdom was born, a realm rising from the ashes of tyranny. Eormen crowned himself and his brother Jotar as kings, vowing to rebuild from the ruins. Illric, now a legend among the exiled, led them towards the icy peaks, his heart heavy with the burden of a future yet unwritten.


#### Northern dwarves 

In the bleak yet mystical lands of the far north, Esteban and Illric, along with an assembly of escaped slaves and their kin, set out to forge a new destiny. They established a humble fishing settlement Eye of Tahrid, on the shores of the lake revered for its connection to Tahrid, the storm god. The lake, known as the Tear of Tahrid, symbolized their gratitude for the god's protection.

In this newfound haven, far from the rule of Ragdar's kings, Esteban and Illric chose not to wear the mantle of rulers but to be guardians of their people. Illric, esteemed as the chieftain of the newly formed Stormson clan, led not as a sovereign but as a caretaker, nurturing the growth of his community.

This spirit of independence and resilience spread across the Bargahai mountains, reaching the vast expanse of the island of Hvitil and smaller isles like Nordgard and Siren's Call. Families, once stripped of their identities, blossomed into clans, each marking the start of a new epoch in their history. In these remote lands, they began anew, free from the shackles of their past.

As generations passed, the harsh climate of the north sculpted the settlers into a people distinct from their origins. They grew shorter, yet broader and mightier, adapting to the relentless cold and rugged terrain. Bearing the scars of rejection and abandonment by their former kin, they sought to redefine themselves. No longer bound to the identity imposed upon them, they embraced a new name: dwarves.

Over the centuries, four clans rose to prominence, each unique in its culture and strength. The Stormson, known for their unyielding spirit; the Bjornblod, fierce and courageous; the Hammarborg, masters of craftsmanship; and the Eldrar, wise and mystical. 

#### Qi-Yian Trade League

Beyond the Emerald Gate, the journey for many was not yet completed. Driven by a thirst for exploration, they travelled further westward, drawn to the shimmering shores of the Westglade Ocean. It was here, in 1376 BG, that they established Mendalis, a modest settlement that would grow into today's city of Segmut, revered as the sea pearl.

Rapidly, Mendalis emerged as a pivotal trading hub in the Westglade. To the north of Mendalis, the Yuzumu shipyard was founded, harnessing the forests around to refine the art of shipbuilding. Both Mendalis and Yuzumu were governed by chosen leaders - governors, their safety entrusted to a modest contingent of guards responsible for safeguarding the bustling trade routes and ships.
As prosperity blossomed within these cities, so too did the threats they faced. Pirates dominated the ocean, while thieves and corrupt officials lurked within the cities' shadows. The need for a stronger defence became evident. Thus, the cities began with construction of walls and fortifications, and the formation of the City Guard, later known as The Whites, was initiated, funded by taxes for their sustenance.

In these developing cities, guilds began to take shape. In Mendalis, the pearl traders, flourishing in wealth, held sway over key decisions. In Yuzumu, the influence rested with the woodworkers and shipbuilders. Recognizing their connected fates, the cities united under the Melandis Trade League in 1320 BG. Open borders and the promise of new beginnings attracted people from diverse lands, transforming the cities into melting pots of various races and a neutral ground for peace talks and negotiations among kingdoms.

To the south of Melandis, elven scouts stumbled upon a settlement of an unknown race. The Shalari, with their unique architecture and language, welcomed them into Daksun. These captivating beings, akin to Moon elves but with skin tones ranging from gray to dark purple, possessed amphibious traits, their necks covered with gills for underwater breathing. The Shalari's architectural prowess was showcased in Daksun, with its towering buildings with marble arches and a majestic magical lighthouse atop the highest coastal hill. This beacon guided Shalari swimmers and boats through the dangerous nights and storms. Their vessels, smaller and swifter than those of the Trade League, were renowned, especially the Colony, a colossal ship-city. It was famous for its ability to vanish at will, evading pirates and foreign fleets.

After three centuries, Daksun would join the ranks of the Trade League, solidifying its position as the third and final major city to become part of this alliance. 

#### Zenari Empire 

Goddess Inis, the intelligent manipulator, took advantage of the fighting in the lands of Ekresh, promising the tribes new lives in a vision of renewal. She promised she would lead the tribes towards a future untainted by the constant shadow of existence. High Priest Kan Mon’ah, of the Ixil tribe, was chosen by her to be her right hand and the High Priest of the new tribe.
Around the year of 300 BG, she united the fighting tribes under one banner and one name, Inis Protectorate. As the new Inis Protectorate grew, a new chapter in the history of the southern tribes opened. Temples, magnificent in their size and structure, emerged from the very earth, standing as monuments of the newfound alliance. Inis most zealous followers, priests and initiates were guided and educated inside the temple studies and laboratories. 

With an extreme momentum, other gods surged forth, following the path paved by Inis. Zachar, god of air, ascended to command over the Hel and K’izik tribes. Meanwhile, the gentle Avelia, cast her persuasive spell over the Mayek tribe, gently molding their aspirations to align with the master plan of Inis. And then there was Jordeg, a figure of resolute determination, whose mastery over land resonated deeply within the Dulgum tribe. As his steady hand took hold, the Dulgum tribe found itself following and supporting him, their strengths converging to fortify the new Zenari alliance.
The alliance of elemental gods was born, heralding the dawn of a new era and the birth of the first Zenari Empire. Temples arose all across the empire, from the desolate wastes in the south to the lush jungles in the north. Kan Mon’ah crowned himself the First Emperor of Zenari. He was a respected and merciful ruler, and he ruled the empire all until his death. For one hundred and twenty years, his reign endured as a testament to the union forged by Inis's hand. His successor was Ner’thazal, previous High Priest of the Mayek tribe.   

Other gods were not happy with this new alliance of elemental gods, especially with what they did to Zenari. Rituals were held across the Zenari lands, bringing each tribe closer to the elements, transforming them for the eternity. Malachai, loyal to the beings that created them, rebuked their actions as treasonous, denouncing them as betrayers of the First Pantheon. He urged Lea to call upon the elemental forces to intervene in this growing divine conflict. 

Celestial stage was set, a grand drama of ambition versus responsibility and loyalty, that would shape the world of Ardeo in unthinkable ways for generations to come. 

#### Elven States

Yio and Xantis were observing the growth and prosperity of the Elven city states. Neither enticed by the attractiveness of elemental domains. Their research led them to study the celestial bodies and their roles in the existence of the planet. Yio was the first to start bridging the gap between the celestial and material realm.  She appeared on many occasions in the material realm, contacting bright individuals, and guiding their research towards the Sun and its influence on the world. Her focus remained mostly north, on cities of Altamere and Galadhal, with a handful of other smaller cities in the northern part of the Elven territory. Elves soon realized the immense power they could gain from the Sun, which led them to establish the Sun Academy in Altamere, and rename the city to Solarion. Sun Academy was the center of research on celestial bodies and everything divine. In the honor of Yio, they raised monuments across the city and named their first council of elders, Council of Yio. 

Xantis shared interests Yio had for the celestial bodies, however he was more into the ones that were circling the planet of Ardeo. Two moons, and an asteroid belt formed from the remains of the third moon, could be seen clearly during most of the nights. Following the path of Yio, Xantis also crossed the border between the realms, finding himself in the city of Nezereth. Amidst the glowing skies, the first moon, Setareh, cast a tranquil and serene influence upon the world below. Its silvery glow, beautiful and calm, guided weary travellers and granted peace and solace to those who sought its soothing light. Ships found their way under its gentle radiance, and those with capable minds harnessed its celestial power. 

Senero, on the other side, had a different influence. Xantis, driven by a pursuit of knowledge, delved into the secrets held by this celestial giant. After spending time on it, the revelations brought forth were unsettling. Some who delved into its mysteries lost their sanity, others underwent inexplicable transformations, changing shapes when the moon was visible high in the sky. Recognizing the peril that lay within the power of Senero, Xantis forbade the studies of the moon in the city of Nezereth. A faction of elves chose to part ways, and leave the city, to continue studies on their own. The birth of Senero Caliphate marked a divergence of paths in the future to be Moon Elf society. One of the most loyal students of Xantis, Cainan Moonborn, led the Senero elves, proclaiming himself as the First Caliph of the Senero Caliphate. With part of their people leaving east, and the ever growing Zenari Empire to the south-west, leaders of the remaining cities urged to sign an agreement of cooperation. With the respect of the will of Xantis, keeping the ban on studies of Senero, they formed Setareh Federation, with the council of the city elders leading it. First elder council was made of Alina Silversong of the city of Nezereth, Meneos Moonshadow of the city of Sarion and Zanaya the Windwhisperer, representing the city of Nur-Eman.
