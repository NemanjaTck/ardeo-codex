The Society of Summanir was distinct from other races across the world. Considering themselves the most advanced and intelligent race, they paid little attention to the planet they inhabited or the gods that created them. Instead, they drew power from both the arcana and Baramul, using it to fuel and expand their city.
However, their population growth was slow, primarily due to the harsh Magus Trials held for all children at a young age. Those who passed the trials remained in the city, bringing pride to their families and becoming mage initiates ready to serve both the city and the nation of Summanir. But for those who failed, the outcome was far worse. Viewed as a disgrace to their families, they were exiled into the unforgiving desert of Luvani. Legends tell that the survivors could be counted on the fingers of one hand.

The Summanir are tall and lean, standing even taller than Elves, with an average height of around 2.10 meters. Their skin tones vary from light brown to nearly black. Despite their height, they are not physically strong, with their lean bodies having almost no muscle. One of their most distinctive features is their hands, which have only two fingers. However, the most noticeable trait is their third eye, located on the upper part of their forehead. This eye is completely blind and often covered by clothing or long hats. Despite its blindness, the third eye serves as a spellcasting focus, and it grants them the ability for telepathy and mental connections with other members of their race.

Around 2700 BG, a young mage named Noemi, one of the exiles, managed to survive the Luvani desert and make her way to the Zenari settlements in the south. Driven by her desire for revenge, she grew stronger and eventually returned to Sheturan. She used the greed Summanir had for the power gained from Baramul, to persuade them to increase the power surge, and exploit Baramul even more.
Although highly intelligent, the Summanir's thirst for power blinded them. They upgraded their connection with Baramul, fabricating more power bridges to the point of collapse. This reckless act destroyed the moon, sending massive fragments crashing down on the city of Sheturan. In a single night, the city was obliterated, with only Noemi and a few others surviving the initial impact.
It is believed that the survivors had no children, leading to the extinction of the entire race.
