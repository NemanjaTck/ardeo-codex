With the elven fleet in disarray following his previous victory, Admiral Morrgataur advanced towards Galadhal Bay. On the evening of the 21st of Leod, 2 BG, he finally arrived, facing the last stand of the Elven fleet. Admiral Neithara awaited him aboard the "New Dawn," the final Celarion flagship. Though outnumbered, these defenders were determined to protect the innocents of Galadhal from the Zenari onslaught. Morrgataur, the great strategist, calculated that he had just one day before the Dwarf and Human fleets would arrive—an oversight that would prove costly.

Despite the unfavorable winds of Avelia, the Stormson clan, devoted to Tahrid, god of storms and thunder, succeeded in driving the combined fleets of Stormson and Hammarborg toward Galadhal. As the Zenari ships stealthily entered the bay under the cover of darkness, their outlines barely visible against the backdrop of the glowing city and the Sun Elf fleet, a distant flash of thunder announced an approaching storm. The only sounds were the soft movements of water against the hulls, until the silence was shattered by a lone horn, followed by the rhythmic pounding of drums piercing the night.

Captain Aehekatl, from the rear line, was the first to notice the shadows of sails through the storm's veil. He quickly signaled for the fleet to adjust course. What initially appeared as a few ships rapidly multiplied, revealing over a hundred vessels bearing the flags of the Stormson and Hammarborg clans. They surged forward on the towering waves of the North Stjarna Sea, led by the fierce Borin Stormson aboard the "Iron Glory," the flagship of the Dwarven fleet.

Morrgataur, recognizing his miscalculation, chose not to surrender but to engage. Realizing he would soon be encircled, he boldly maneuvered his fleet to sail directly into the storm. Admiral Neithara, quick to realize his intentions, commanded her fleet to give chase.

As the Zenari ships neared the Dwarven fleet, the first volley of flaming rocks hurled from catapults began to rain down upon them. The two fleets collided amidst chaos. For over three hours, the Zenari fought valiantly against overwhelming odds. Despite their fierce resistance, they were ultimately outnumbered and outmatched. That day marked the devastating loss of the Zenari fleet; many of its warriors perished, others were captured, and the body of Admiral Morrgataur was never recovered.

The Battle of Galadhal Bay thus became the first significant victory for the allied forces of Elves, Humans, and Dwarves. In the days following, as the fleets of Ragdar and Moravia landed in Galadhal, the leaders of these factions forged an alliance, naming it Amalgam. This coalition would later be joined by the Orcs from the south, marking an important point in the region’s history and setting the united front against the Zenari Empire.
