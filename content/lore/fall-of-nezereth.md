On the eastern front, following the conquest of Nur-Eman, Ner’thazal swiftly established a temporary government, subjecting the city's population to enslavement. At the same time, Admiral Morrgataur led the fleet northward towards the strategic city of Galadhal, aiming to encircle Solarion, leaving behind only a handful of transport ships to reinforce the Zenari troops arriving from across Volandis.

Nezereth, the largest city of the Setareh Federation, stood proudly in a valley across the River Lei Santis, a three days' march from Nur-Eman. The city was famed for its magnificent architecture designed to harness and reflect moonlight, rendering the city invisible at night. Ner’thazal, confident and calculating, planned a swift conquest to capture Nezereth within a day, before the nightfall could allow the defenders to regroup.

The defense of Nezereth was led by Raunas Nightveil, one of the elders of the Setareh Federation. Despite the incoming danger, he refused to retreat to Solarion, choosing instead to lead the city's defenses. Armed with two weary Sun Elf regiments and approximately 5,000 Moon Elf troops—comprising spearmen, archers, mages, and a modest contingent of siege equipment—Raunas faced an overwhelming force of nearly 20,000 troops commanded by Ner’thazal himself. The odds were against him, with reinforcements far from reach.

On the morning of the 18th of Issha, 2 BG, the Zenari commenced their siege with an intense bombardment. Despite Nezereth being completely encircled by enemy forces on land and river, Raunas had strategically ordered the demolition of all suburban areas around the city days prior, eliminating potential cover for the attackers. With troops being in the open, and heavy protection from Moon Elf mages, Nezereth walls were not match for the Zenari attacks. Frustrated by the failure of his plans, Ner’thazal dispatched a messenger to Shahtani, who was leading southern troops towards Sarion, urging her to reorganize and reinforce his besieging forces.

Recognizing the dire situation as Shahtani’s troops neared, Raunas understood that their end is near. There was no option to meet them in the open field, being outnumbered five to one. When Shahtani’s reinforcements arrived, the Zenari ranks counted to about 35,000, Ner’thazal launched a full-scale attack, employing his secret Hel cannons for continuous bombardment, supporting his ground troops as they surged towards the city walls. The defenders, however, stood resilient, their mages casting protective shields and disrupting the attackers' ladders, causing substantial Zenari casualties. Realizing the futility of his efforts, even with air support from airships and Roc riders, Ner’thazal ordered a retreat. On that day, Zenari forces suffered heavy losses, approximately 8,000, compared to less than a thousand on the defender's side—a resounding victory for Nezereth that bolstered their hope under Raunas's leadership.

However, Shahtani, ever resourceful, developed a cunning new strategy. She directed the strongest Earth Zenari units to begin tunneling under the cover of night towards Nezereth’s walls, exploiting the elves' perceived security. After three nights, their subterranean efforts reached the city's fortifications.

In the dawn of the twelfth day of the siege, as Raunas prepared to repel yet another frontal assault, the ground beneath the city walls suddenly gave way. The tunnels had undermined the foundations, causing a catastrophic collapse that crushed the defenders above. Within the hour, Zenari troops flooded through the breached walls. Taking the city, though more resource-intensive and time-consuming than anticipated, was a personal victory for Ner’thazal, who, in a display of brutal retribution, flayed Raunas alive in the city square and enslaved the surviving populace. Now, he paused to recuperate and reinforce his armies, preparing for the next phase of his campaign.
