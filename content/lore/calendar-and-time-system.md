The Sun Elves, inspired by the teachings of Yio and their newfound understanding of celestial bodies, began documenting the history of the world they live in. They devised a comprehensive system for measuring time and recording the passage of years.
Soon, the Sun calendar was introduced, and it marked the progression of time with remarkable precision. A year in the calendar comprised 300 days, every year further divided into four seasons. This allowed them to not only record the history, but to track the changing of seasons.

The cataclysmic events of the War of the Elements, following the sundering of the continent and end of the war, changed civilizations to their roots. To mark the moment that ended the suffering and remind all races and all of life on the misfortune that war brought on them, they chose to reset the year count, to begin from the exact day of sundering and the end of the war. 

300 days a year, 10 months, 3 tendays in a month, each month named by a god.
