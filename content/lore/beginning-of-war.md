In the metropolises of the Zenari Empire, elven spies began to detect unsettling signs of escalating military activity. Ner’Thazal, seizing the moment, journeyed to the strategic city of Ulund'ari, rallying forces from Maanar'Kin and warriors from Ekresh lands. His assembled army, numbering over 20,000 Zenari troops, was strenghten by 500 elementals and an array of siege engines ready for battle. At the same time, Shahtani ventured southeast, to Dogbor, leading generals from the Hel tribes alongside mages of the Arcana Zenari. Together with the stalwart Earth Zenari soldiers, they amassed a nearly 15,000-strong army, supported by close to 300 elementals. Meanwhile, near the city of Aghanat, a smaller contingent of 5,000 Ocean Zenari, supplemented by a few elementals and arbalests, readied themselves to advance northward towards the human cities.
Once ready, they marched for the river Valdanis.

In response to the escalating threat, the Setareh Federation swiftly sought reinforcements from allied elven factions. The Senero Caliphate, recognizing the incoming danger, decided to fortify its own borders, declining any military assistance to the Federation. In stark contrast, Solarion reacted with speed; one of their most esteemed generals, Alerius Sunshield, arrived with elite battalions of Sun Elf guards and archers. Further bolstering their defense, Solarion dispatched a segment of their fleet to reinforce the strategic naval position of Nur-Eman along the river Valdanis.

The defenders, however, were ill-prepared for the onslaught launched in their direction. Under the direct command of Inis, Zenari mages were armed with an arsenal of destructive spells. Zenari readied their light, swift ships—each manned by around 50 sailors and warriors, and one to three mages—for a surprise attack. On the night of the 7th day of Ishha, 2 BG, the skies over Nur-Eman ignited in flames. The elven fleet, caught off guard by the ferocity of the assault, was overwhelmed, losing control of the sea in a matter of hours. Despite the heavy bombardment from the Zenari warships, the harbor held out valiantly. Yet, by dawn, Zenari forces had begun landing troops along the coastlines, effectively besieging Nur-Eman from all directions. The city, tragically, fell before any additional reinforcements could reach it, resulting in the total annihilation of its defenders.

As word of Nur-Eman’s fall spread, the allied elven troops began to congregate in Nezereth, the Federation's largest city. Moon Elves dispatched scouts to the northern human and dwarven cities, seeking assistance and warning of the Zenari empire's ambition for total world domination. However, with the Zenari armies advancing swiftly towards Nezereth, the grim reality settled in: help would not arrive in time.
