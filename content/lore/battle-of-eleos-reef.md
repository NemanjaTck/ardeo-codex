Admiral Morrgataur, a seasoned captain, had spent his entire life navigating the seas. Born in a modest fishing village along the western coast of Westglade, he was familiar with the ocean from a young age, having accompanied his father on countless fishing expeditions. His naval career began in Aghanat, a bustling port city in the far east, even before the Zenari adopted their elemental rituals.

Assigned by Ner’thazal to secure the Galadhal Bay and block any northern reinforcements, Morrgataur was aware of the challenges ahead. He understood that a direct confrontation with the Sun Elf fleet on the open sea would likely end in disaster, given the Zenari fleet's lighter, more agile ships were outmatched in strength and numbers.

Strategically, Morrgataur reached the Eleos reef, at the northwest point of the Eurenis continent, and cleverly used its shallow waters and jagged rocky formations to conceal the majority of his fleet. The remainder he docked a few miles away. For seven days, his mages worked tirelessly, manipulating the winds and weather to keep their ships hidden, while he executed a deceptive maneuver—quietly reinforcing his visible fleet with a few ships from the hidden ones during daylight, creating the illusion of a steadily growing force.

On the morning of the eighth day, the Sun Elf fleet appeared on the horizon. Their scouts reported only a small contingent of Zenari ships at Eleos reef. Eager to eliminate what they perceived as a vulnerable target before it could receive more reinforcements, the Sun Elves decided to strike swiftly. Seizing the opportunity, Morrgataur orchestrated a retreat with the smaller segment of his fleet, luring the Sun Elves into the treacherous shallow waters.

With the Sun Elves’ flank exposed and their ships struggling in the adverse conditions, the hidden Zenari fleet struck. Zenari mages unleashed storms and summoned fierce winds, severely hampering the Sun Elves' ability to maneuver their larger vessels in the shallow reef. The ensuing chaos was catastrophic for the Sun Elf fleet, which lost three Celarions—their esteemed flagships—and several smaller ships, while many of their sailors and soldiers drowned during the battle.

The aftermath was devastating, with estimates suggesting that around 3,000 elven sailors and soldiers perished that day. Morrgataur's strategic brilliance not only resulted in minimal losses for his fleet but also secured a staggering victory. Powered by this triumph and needing little reinforcement, he set sail a few days later, continuing his journey eastward towards Galadhal Bay, with the winds of success at his back.
