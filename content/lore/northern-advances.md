In a few days, the initial two armies, led by Ner’thazal himself, surged across the Volandis and penetrated deep into the elven territories. Meanwhile, Ner’thazal's ambitious second-in-command, General Zaeor, led the K’uhad regiments northward towards the ancient city of Nohenjo Daro. The strategy was clear: Zaeor was to hold his forces at the ready, awaiting the early triumphs on the eastern front before rallying additional troops to push north into Luvani.

However, Zaeor, a young and impulsive commander with a fiery spirit, was eager to carve out his own legend under the Zenari banners. Disregarding the experienced counsel of the high priests Kun’dar and Ragaza, who accompanied him, Zaeor chose a path of haste over caution. Merely three days after the forces of Ner'thazal had traversed the Volandis, he led his troops into Luvani, driven by a desire to demonstrate his valor and tactical prowess.

The journey that followed was grueling. Over the span of ten days, Zaeor's forces battled not only the enemy but also the relentless conditions of the harsh desert landscape. Exhausted yet undeterred, the Zenari legions finally arrived at the formidable walls of Nohenjo Daro, poised for a siege that would test the limits of their endurance and resolve.
