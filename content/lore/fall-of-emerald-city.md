Determined that the setback at Nohenjo Daro would not define his military career, Zaeor regrouped, refusing to allow this defeat to mark the end of his campaign. In a strategic move to limit the city's resources, he dispatched several units to raid the caravansaries surrounding Nohenjo Daro. However, this effort was swiftly ended by the Kadir Vanguard Wyrm riders, who pursued and decimated his raiding parties. Faced with this fierce resistance, Zaeor was forced to retreat back into the jungles.

After spending a month regrouping and reinforcing his ranks, Zaeor launched a renewed offensive. This time, he had a different course in mind: his army emerged from the jungle and proceeded northward along the Orqon mountain chain. In less than five days, his forces arrived at Emerald Crossing and laid siege to the beautiful Emerald City. The elven defenders, overwhelmed by the sheer strength of the Zenari army, stood no chance; the city capitulated swiftly.

However, the city's surrender did not quench Zaeor's thirst for vengeance. Still bitter from his previous defeat, he led his most loyal and formidable warriors into the heart of Emerald City. In a grim display of ruthless vengeance, Zaeor orchestrated a massacre, systematically slaughtering every inhabitant in cold blood. The city itself was set ablaze, its beauty consumed by flames.

He spared a handful of survivors, sending them as messengers to all human kingdoms. These few traumatized souls were tasked with spreading the word of the atrocities committed at Emerald City, a calculated move by Zaeor to sow fear and despair across the lands. His message was clear: he was a force to be reckoned with, one that would not hesitate to invoke horror to achieve his ends.
