The Elves were desperately trying to evacuate and save as many people as possible without directly engaging the Zenari armies. However, Ner’thazal and his forces, even with siege weapons, advanced swiftly, covering 25 miles per day. An open field battle became inevitable.
The Elven army was no match for the 30,000 Zenari troops marching toward them, so they sought a terrain advantage. The small hills overlooking the Hazimat Fields seemed like a good defensive position to hold off the enemy and buy time for the evacuation of nearby villages and towns to Solarion.
Due to the war emergency, the Council of Yio granted full command of the city and its army to the battle-hardened General Thenasil Lightsworn. Thenasil knew his mission: to save as many lives as possible by retreating them to Solarion, while waiting for allied forces to arrive. With only 10,000 troops under his command, he understood that leaving Solarion defenseless was not an option, but neither was winning a direct confrontation against the much larger Zenari army. The only choice was to take part of his forces and meet the Zenari at Hazimat Fields to buy time.
The speech he gave that day is still recorded in the books of Solarion’s libraries:
“Protectors of Solarion! Evil is near! Darkness has swallowed the lands of our brethren, and now it comes for us. Today, I call you! Ride with me! Ride and face the darkness! Let the light of Yio give us strength! Yio Ovesil! Yio Ovesil! Yio Ovesil!”
*Yio Ovesil*, meaning "Yio’s blessing" in Elvish, remains a cultural phrase to this day and is used by Sun Elves when they greet one another.

Thenasil led his best 2,000 knights, archers, and mages to Hazimat Fields, leaving his second-in-command, Lianti Salorius, to lead the defense should anything happen to him. Thenasil’s forces arrived at Hazimat Fields first, a place known for its fertile soil. From the small hills overlooking the fields, they could see the once live Sun Elf settlements, now evacuated and empty. After setting up barricades on the hills, all that was left to do was wait for the Zenari army.
As evening approached, the first distant torches appeared, quickly spreading across the horizon until they encircled the entire length of Hazimat Fields. The stillness of the warm night was shattered by the sound of war drums in the distance. Thenasil looked at his warriors and saw fear in their eyes as they faced the massive Zenari army. Standing before them with courage, he knew what they were fighting for.

On the other side, Ner’thazal led the Zenari troops. His scouts had reported the presence of the Elven regiment in their way and reinforcements from Human and Dwarven armies expected to arrive in a day from Galadhal. Unwilling to wait, Ner’thazal divided his army into three parts to try and encircle the Elves on the hills. E’zuhl the Red took the riders to the left, while Rohgdarg Stonehill led part of the army to the right.

Thenasil and his troops waited for the Zenari to approach, ready to unveil their barricades and flaming traps. The battle began. After the first hour or two of spells and volleys of arrows, the Zenari forces finally managed to encircle Thenasil and his men. The melee combat was devastating for the Zenari, but their sheer numbers began to overwhelm the Elves. Thenasil formed a spear shield to protect his mages and archers, leading his swordsmen into close combat himself. Survivors later recounted how Thenasil, wielding his glowing glaive, danced through the moonlight, cutting down Zenari one by one.

As the night wore on, Thenasil saw his warriors falling around him and knew they would soon be wiped out with no chance of retreat. Then, he heard a sound in the distance, a horn he had never heard before, the Horn of Moravia. General Ladazar Nemov, leading the elite Stag Knights and supported by Gaia druids and rangers, had arrived at Hazimat Fields, traveling through the forests to evade Zenari scouts.

The first volley of arrows and thorns flashed through the night, striking the Zenari riders on the left flank. E’zuhl attempted to turn his forces to meet the threat, but it was too late. The Stag Knights charged with incredible force, obliterating the Zenari riders and killing E’zuhl in the process. Seeing this as an opportunity, Thenasil ordered the remnants of his troops to retreat. Ladazar and his forces helped cover the Elves’ retreat, allowing them to return to Solarion.

The Battle of Hazimat Fields was lost, but the primary goal was achieved. The people had been evacuated to Solarion, and the allied forces had arrived to strengthen the city’s defenses. Thenasil returned with only 200 of his warriors, most in no condition to fight, while Ladazar also lost many of his knights. However, the Zenari suffered far greater losses, with estimates ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 troops, as evidenced by the weakened force that laid siege to Solarion a few days later.
