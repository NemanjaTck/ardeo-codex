Ragdar is a militaristic kingdom ruled by two kings, each presiding over their own capital. Magic is strictly forbidden, and practitioners caught using it are sentenced to death. The humans of Ragdar are shorter and stronger than others of their race, considered direct ancestors of the Dwarves. In the north, the fortified city of Helburg lies in the mountains, enclosed by stone city walls that extend for several kilometers. Helburg is renowned for its infantry and siege weaponry. To the south, nestled in the valley of rivers, lies Ullheim, the kingdom’s second capital, known for having some of the world's best archers and cavalry archers. Each city and village maintains its own guard and marshals, who pledge allegiance to both kings. In Ragdar, the ruling principle is that while one king leads the army in war, the other remains to prepare defenses and manage the kingdom.

##### Life

Ragdar is led by dual kings and advised by a Council of Elders, comprised of war veterans. In times of war, typically male-dominated roles such as merchants, artists, fishermen, hunters, and barmaids are often filled by women. From an early age, children undergo strict discipline and begin brutal training at age 5. By age 10, they face their first trial, the **Trial of Strength**, where they combat specially bred animals. This annual event is one of the kingdom’s largest, organized and overseen by the king. Boys demonstrating skill are placed into small, elite units known as Einheit, which train and fight together, forming the backbone of Ragdar’s feared military. Not all boys become warriors; roles are assigned based on skills and include blacksmiths, leatherworkers, healers, alchemists, cavalry riders, and archers. While girls can participate in the trial, it is less common due to the equal treatment they receive as boys.

The **Start of Winter** marks another significant holiday in Ragdar, celebrating Eisera, the Goddess of Winter. Though Ragdar is not known for its art galleries, its culture deeply respects the arts, immortalizing its greatest fighters and heroes in songs, stories, and paintings. Some Ragdarian authors are noted for their military tactic books and war poems.

##### Diplomacy

The Ragdar Kingdom is perpetually at war with the northern Dwarven clans, who often raid weaker villages or the coast. Ragdar’s offensives into Dwarven territories have generally been unsuccessful.

To the northwest lies the port town of Worenhavn, which was the largest port on the continent's western side until it was besieged by Clans Bjornblod and Eldrar ten years before the War of the Elements. When aid from Helburg and Ullheim was delayed, the town's governor sought help from a shadowy mage, resulting in a sky of green fire and the dead rising from their graves to repel the invaders. After the Dwarves retreated, the undead turned on Worenhavn’s citizens, eradicating its population. To this day, the streets of Worenhavn remain eerily silent, haunted by an omnipresent malevolence, with no human or dwarf daring to enter.

##### Important Characters

- Arvidias the Second, king, residing in Ulfheim
    Old, wise and treacherous, ex-general, trying to turn Ragdar from duarchy to monarchy

- Friedrich Arbach, king, residing in Helburg
    Good, resolute man, called the Iron Fist among his people. Younger and stronger than Arvidias, however not as bright.

- Leoric Rothwolf, general and world renown swordsman
    General that led the Ragdar army in the battle of Hazimat fields and Siege of Solarion. Her was the one who ordered sneak attack from Moravia forces through the forest to surprise and delay Zenari army.

- Anneliese Drechler, Ulfheim city guard leader and epic poems writer
    Leader of the city guard in Ulfheim, one of the greatest warriors of Ragdar Kingdom. She is also known for writing poems, creating a place in history for many forgotten legends and warriors of the kingdom.
