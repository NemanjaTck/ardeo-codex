The desolate lands of Ekresh, once lush and vibrant plains, were devastated by natural forces. Tectonic movements from the core of Ardeo led to the formation of an active volcano, which the Zenari named Vuldrush. The once prosperous southern tribes, skilled in agriculture on Ekresh's fertile lands, faced dire circumstances as resources dwindled. The Zenari, previously united, split into two tribes: Ixil and Atash. It was Inis, the Goddess of Fire, who united them under the Inis Protectorate, initiating them as Flame Zenari.

Before the War of the Elements, Inis taught them to cultivate unique plant species and to harness the volcanic wasteland's potential. In their two largest cities, Chandar and Ra’und, immense forges were constructed, leading to advancements in metallurgy and craftsmanship. They became masters of fire and metal. During the war, the Flame Zenari were among the most formidable troops of the Zenari Empire. Many perished, following Inis blindly, never questioning the will of the gods.

After the war and the dissolution of the Zenari Empire, the Inis Protectorate managed to preserve its territories. The new High Priest, Mag’and, opened trade routes to other Zenari lands and to the Qi-Yian Trade League in the northwest. She implemented a policy of open borders, welcoming other cultures to visit and settle within the Inis Protectorate.

##### Life

The Inis Protectorate, like other Zenari states, is a form of theocracy. Being a priest is a highly respected position, not attainable by just anyone. Priests make all significant decisions. While local laws and decisions are administered among priests based on rank, critical decisions about external relations and overall governance are entrusted to the High Priest, the head of state.

**The Flaming Hammer**: Across the Inis Protectorate, in addition to respect for priests, there is profound reverence for blacksmiths. The highest accolade for a living blacksmith is the title of "The Flaming Hammer."

**Volcano Eruptions**: The people of the Protectorate do not view the eruptions of Vuldrush as bad omens. Instead, both Chandar and Ra’und celebrate these eruptions as signs of good fortune. Familiar with the volcano's behavior, they can predict eruptions and enhance their beauty by positioning their strongest mages at the summit of Vuldrush.


##### Diplomacy

Following the war, the Inis Protectorate enacted an open borders law, allowing individuals of any race and origin to enter the previously secluded lands of Ekresh. With the fall of the Zenari Empire, the Protectorate, under its new leadership, is now focused on trade and land development. Efforts are being made to improve relations with Human kingdoms, although diplomatic interactions with Elves remain somewhat reserved. The war ended in a stalemate, and the memories of what the occupying Zenari forces did in Elven cities have not been forgotten.

- Arod the Relentless, Royal Guard
    One of the surviving generals of Zenari Empire forces, now leading the royal guard units of the High Priestess

    He was one of the leaders of the Flame Zenari initiation, too much of a coward to be a part of the war. He is unhappy with how the war ended, and is one of the few plotting a return of the Zenari Empire. He is located in a small temple in the Vudrush volcano
