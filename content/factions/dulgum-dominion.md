Living on the southwestern rocky coasts of the continent of Westglade, the Zenari have quickly honed a profound understanding of the earth and its minerals. Dogbor, the second oldest city on the continent, was established by Zenari who discovered the ground rich in various metals and minerals near a massive crater. This discovery led to the development of smaller mining and quarry settlements around Dogbor. These communities have maintained their ancient Zenari traditions, placing great value on their heritage and origins.

##### Life

In Dulgum, tradition is held in high esteem, and a family's name carries significant weight. Descendants from longstanding families, some of which have existed for hundreds of years, are often reserved for better professions and positions. There has been only one instance in the history of the Dulgum Dominion where the chief came from a relatively newer family. Typically, marriages within the community are preferred to strengthen bonds and alliances between families, and arranged marriages are still common in Dulgum Dominion.

Dulgum Dominion is fiercely protective of its territory, boasting a vast array of keeps and watchtowers that encircle all of its settlements. Dogbor, in particular, is considered one of the toughest cities to siege and conquer due to its natural advantages and fortifications.

Also, the Dulgum Dominion is renowned for its annual war games, a bloody, barehand wrestling competition attracting contestants worldwide.

##### Diplomacy

Dulgum Dominion maintains strong relationships with other Zenari states while keeping a reserved and distant stance toward other factions. They hold a respect for elves due to their shared heritage and longevity but do not significantly engage with other races. Following the war, they continue to adhere to the teachings of Jordeg and his earth-centric philosophies.
