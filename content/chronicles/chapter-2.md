**Entering the Gray Forest**

Next to the Woodcutter's Lodge, a small path leads into the Gray Forest. The group will enter, searching for clues, missing people, or creatures in golden armor. As they venture deeper into the woods, they will encounter an old watchtower. The old watchtower is about a day and a half's walk from Holzenar. If the players travel during the day, for every four hours of travel, you can have the party roll for stealth and roll one d12 on the following encounter table. In total, you should have maximum three encounters during the first day, and one in the second day.
Stealth average roll:
- under 10 - Party is surprised if it is attacked by bandits or beasts
- 10 - 15 - Party can move unseen
- 15+ - Party can move unseen and will surprise enemy if they attack

Encounter table:

| Roll  | Encounter                                                                            |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 12    | Wounded man that can point them to the Watchtower, or nothing happens                |
| 10-11 | Circle of Gaia spies, scouting the area, players need Persuasion, or nothing happens |
| 8-9   | Poison berries, 4 are goodberry, 6 are poisonus, nature DC 13 check                  |
| 6-7   | Bandit group - 3 thugs and 1 scout                                                   |
| 4-5   | Beast group - 3 wolves and 1 bear                                                    |
| 2-3   | 2 specters                                                                           |
| 1     | Giant boar                                                                           |

As the party approaches the old watchtower in the distance and heads towards it, they need to navigate through two different traps.

**Hidden pit**. Ask who is leading the group. Group leader should succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom (Perception) role. If they are actively looking for the traps, DC is 14. If they fail, they will step on a rope, opening the pit. The pit is a hole, 20 feet in radius.
**Hidden net**. Ask who is leading the second part of the way. Group leader should succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom (Perception) role. If they are actively looking for the traps, DC is 14. If they fail, net will activate and pull them into the air. Net has 20ft radius.

If the party fails to detect either trap, moments after being caught, before they can start climbing or cutting the net, an old wizard in a golden cloak (Eneelaidas) will appear with a few other guards in golden armor and cast Sleep on all members of the party.

> Old watchtower is essentially two watch towers connected with a tunnel and a wall. It was built before the War of the Elements and partially destroyed by an invoked storm that also killed a big part of the vegetation and turned the green lush forest to a gray and dull environment.

If the party spots the traps and proceeds towards the watchtower, they will see only one creature in golden armor in front of what appears to be a side entrance to the tower. By killing or distracting the guard, they can proceed into the watchtower.

**Old watchtower**

Depending on whether they were caught, the party will either wake up in a prison cell in the first room or enter through the door. The watchtower is inhabited, but it is no longer as functional as it once was. What was previously a multi-floor structure now has only a ground floor, with rubble and remnants of the upper floors scattered around the towers.

**Ceiling**. The stone ceiling is around 10 feet high.

**Walls**. The walls are heavily fortified, even in this ruined state. Small holes, large enough for rats and small rodents, have likely been formed over time or as a result of the war.

**Light**. Unless otherwise noted, all keyed locations on the map are lit by torches.

> Coming inside, strong stench of dead, rotten corpses overcomes you. You can see corpses and bones laying around, with rats and insects feasting on them. Stone wall is standing still, with few holes inside of it, covered in dried blood for the most part of it. There is a stone door to the east.

1. Improvised Jail

> This entire room is the ground floor of one of the towers. The bars and hinges are in relatively new condition, suggesting that the cell is improvised and that this jail wasn’t here previously. Bones and body remains are scattered around the oval room.

  If the party was caught, they will wake up inside the prison in this room; otherwise, they will enter from the door in the northwest. In either case, there is only one guard, a Golden Hand Recruit, a young orc, who is sleeping. Inside the prison, sitting in a corner, is a small dwarf named Fritz Ebon. He is locked here, utterly terrified, and somewhat mad. He has heard screams at night, along with the guards' stories of mutilations and experiments on the captured prisoners.
  Even if the party unlocks the jail cell, he refuses to leave. He mentions having seen a beautiful woman in a golden cloak, passing through the halls and giving orders to the golden-armored guards. (_This should hint that Eneelaidas is not who they appear to be._)
  Opening the door to the east, by taking keys from the guard, the party can see a long hallway.

2. Hallway

>The hallway is partially lit, with two doors on the left, one door on the right, and a door at the far end of it. Light flickers from the first door on the right, suggesting that the door is open.

**Arrow slit trap**. In the walls between the doors on the sides are small, almost invisible arrow slits. Players must succeed on DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check, to notice the unaligned brick in the floor. On a failure, if they walk through the center of the hall, they will step on the brick and activate the slits. Characters must succeed on a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw. On a failure, receive 1d6 damage. On a successful save, players receive only half of the damage.

3. Blood hall

> Door to the right of the hallway is slightly open. By entering you can see a room full of corpses. Here you can decide how bloody and gore you want to describe this, as you re trying to instil a horror in your players. Far at the end of this hall, there is a old catapult, seemingly non functional, probably leftover from the Ragdar forces before the War of the Elements.

From the rooms on the right, the players can hear the clashing and clinking of swords. Rooms on the left give nothing but complete silence.

4. Training room

>  The pungent smell of sweat overwhelms your senses as you enter this room. The medium-sized room has been converted into a training facility. Training dummies, archery targets, various weapons, shields, and armor line the racks along the sides. Three creatures in golden armor are practicing sword fighting.

2 Golden Hand Recruits and 1 Golden Hand Soldier, are practicing sword-fighting. If they hear sounds from the hall, they will run into it, otherwise they never leave the training room.

**Treasure**: Their weapons, 2d6 gold pieces total, 2d100 silver pieces and a potion of healing.

5. Sleeping room

Next to the training room there is a small sleeping room. Three bedrolls are on the ground. Players must succeed on the DC 13 Intelligence (Investigation) check to notice the Potion of Invisibility under one of the bedrolls.

**Treasure**: Inside second bedroll there is 4d6 gold pieces.

6. Cult room

> Smell of corpse decay gets stronger as you approach the door. Door is not locked. Opening it, you immediately see 2 cultists cutting through already dead bodies, chained on the two wooden stretchers. To the left, there is a well lit statue of Neiri, in black marble, covered with blood. Blood and organs are all across the floor and stretchers. In the farthest part of the room, behind the black marble table, there is Agamand - a hunched old Elf (cult fanatic) in the black cloak, and black apron around his waist, stained with blood.

Elf cult leader, named Agamand, greets the party with the words "Oh, more food for my precious pet" and together with two other cultists attacks.

**Treasure**: On the table and on the bookshelves, there are books studying Soul realm and the goddess Neiri. On the Agamand, party can find one platinum ring (25 gold), Scroll of Sanctuary and Scroll of Animate Dead.

7. Spider cell

>   Through a small peephole in the heavily locked wooden door, a character with darkvision can see rubble scattered across the small, dark room. There is no sound emanating from the room, just the smell of rot.

The player can unlock the heavily locked wooden door using thieves' tools with a successful DC 15 Dexterity check. Inside the small, dark room, players can see rubble, and among the pieces of stone, some shining gems. With a successful DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check, players can also notice a sword buried in the rubble. Upon entering the room, the party is attacked by a Neurotoxin Spider lurking on the ceiling.

**Treasure**: 3 malachite gems (15gp each) and a magic longsword +1.

8. Dining Hall entrance

> The wooden tiles creak underfoot as you try to walk silently through the small entry hall. A faint light lingers from slightly open doors leading to another room. By the sounds of chatting and loud eating, you assume it is a dining hall or a common room where soldiers gather.

The players notice wooden tiles on the floor, different from the stone bricks that covered the floor until now. To the right, there are wooden doors, slightly ajar, with light shining through. This small hall is completely dark.

9. Dining Hall

> You enter a large dining room. With wooden floors, tables, shelves, and crates filled with food, wine, and spices, this room looks out of place in the dungeon. At a smaller coffee table near the entrance, two creatures in golden cloaks are playing cards. Two more are seated at the large dining table at the back, loudly chewing on meat.

2 Golden Hand Scouts are sitting and drinking. 2 more Golden Hand Recruits are sitting at the table at the back eating. 2 other Recruits are sleeping in the room next door, and need 2 rounds to join the eventual fight. Scouts and recruits in this room are not that eager to fight. If the players roleplay well, and succeed on a DC 20 Charisma (Persuasion) or (Deception) check, they can convince all four of the Golden Hand members to drop their uniforms and leave the tower.

**Treasure**: 20 days of rations and 10 bottles of fine wine (2 gp each)

10. Main sleeping room 

> More than five bedrolls are spread across the floor of this long room. At the far end, partially separated by a thin wall, you can see a wooden bathing tub, currently empty. It appears that this room serves as both a bedroom and a lavatory.

2 Golden Hand Recruits are sleeping here. They join the fight if the fight erupts in the Dining Hall

**Treasure**: 2d6 gold pieces and 1d100 silver pieces in pouches. 2 longswords are laying on the floor.



11. Armory hall

> Upon opening the door, you see a stone hall with a door on the right side. A figure in golden armor is feeding a wolf.

1 Golden Hand Soldier is feeding a wolf in this empty hall. On the sound of opening of the door, they attack. After the players enter the room, ask for a perception check. If they succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check, they notice a small, uneven brick on the rightside wall. If they push it, small door opens.


12. Weapon room

> A small, cluttered room filled with various weapons. Some of the weapons are sorted in a semblance of order, while other parts are just strewn about, lying on the floor and making movement through the room much more challenging.

**Treasure**: 8 spears, 4 shortswords, 2 longswords, 1 light crossbow, 2 quivers with 20 crossbow bolts each.

13. Hidden room 

> Pressing on an unaligned brick on the wall opens a small stone door, 4 feet tall and just 3 feet wide. A medium-sized character can enter. Inside the small, unlit room, you see two ornate chests, both secured with rusty, weak locks.

Locks on the chests are old and rusty. Players can easily break them and open the chests.

**Treasure**: 4 potions of healing

14. Boss room

 > The door at the end of the hall is not locked. When you open the door and step inside, you find yourself in a similarly shaped oval room, like the improvised prison, with heavy stone walls. Standing and talking in front of the wooden table in the center of the room are two creatures: a strong Zargon Human in golden armor and a Moon Elf in a golden cloak. To the left of them, you notice a stone staircase leading up to a wooden hatch door.

1 Golden Hand Soldier, Zargon Human, Ebenezer Danjuma, leader of the Old Watchtower regiment, is discussing tactics with a Dwarven figure, in a golden cloak (Golden Hand Scout). If the players are wearing golden cloaks or armor, and succeed on a DC 22 Charisma (Deception) check, they could be allowed to leave the tower via stairs on the side of the room. In any other situation, they both attack.

**Treasure**: Cloak of the Dryad, 30g in items, scroll of Hold Person, scroll of Revivify. They also find letters, addressed to Ebenezer, signed by Eneelaidas. Only one is in readable condition and it reads: "Keep disrupting and alarming the forest. Everything goes according to plan."

**Leaving back for Holzenar**

When the players manage to leave the watchtower through the hatch in the roof of the last room, they are immediately met by Zora and Ajaw, who have cleared the guards on that side, allowing the players to quickly escape into the forest.
Zora and Ajaw have found Borik, their compatriot, dead in a wild cemetery near the watchtower. The cemetery is filled with woodcutters, villagers, and a few Circle of Gaia members. They have also learned about the Golden Hand, a global faction of mercenaries, and have discovered a story about the old wizard. In Holzenar, an elderly Halfling lady, the owner of the Remedy Shop, awaits them to share more information.
The road through the forest back to Holzenar should be straightforward, as Zora and Ajaw know the safe paths. They will stop and set up camp for one night, with both players and them taking turns on watch. This presents an opportunity for the player with the Raven Syndicate quest to find a way to steal the green emerald from Zora. No encounters will occur during the night, and the players will safely arrive back at the village. If the players have not reached level 3 yet, they should now advance to level 3.
