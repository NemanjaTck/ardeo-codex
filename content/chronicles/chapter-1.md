**Adventure plot**

Seventy years have passed since the end of the War of the Elements. Leena Desai, a young mage and one of the three chancellors of the Qi-Yian Trade League—a union of three cities on the western coast of Wesglade—now plays a pivotal role. The end of the War of the Elements was marked by the closing of the gates to the Abyss realm. Legends recount that the artifacts used to open these gates were destroyed after the war, all except one, which is located in the southern parts of the Ragdar Kingdom, the northern neighbor of the Trade League. Unknown to her, the artifact she seeks is not an inanimate object but a humanoid: the old Storm Zenari priest, Run'Garad, one of the most powerful priests of the former Zenari Empire.

To acquire the necessary resources for her quest, Leena has secured the support of Segmut, the largest city in the Qi-Yian Trade League. She conceals her true intentions, instead claiming to cooperate with the Ragdar Kingdom on reactivating mines in southern Ragdar. With these resources, she has hired an army of mercenaries—the Golden Hand—and plans to act quickly while the Ragdar Kingdom is preoccupied with fighting the Dwarves in the north. She has learned that a map to the artifact is hidden in ruins within the Gray Forest. This map will lead its finders to an abandoned gold mine northeast of the Gray Forest.

>Leena Desai, lvl 9 wizard. She is one of the three heads of the Qi-Yian Trade League. She is a famous wizard, known to amass huge amounts of treasures in suspicious ways and rise to one of the wealthiest members of the Trade League. With majority of votes on one of the meetings of the Qi-Yian Trade League, she was chosen to replace Takehiro Shen, as one of the brightest and more perspective members. She goes disguised most of the time as the old bearded wizard with the hood, named Eneelaidas - anagram of Leena Desai.

Starting the adventure

Reasons for players coming this far, to the southern parts of Ragdar Kingdom could vary. Some could be on personal mission, other looking to earn some money. You can define character backstories and how they met, if they met before.

**Read this to players:**

>Autumn, beginning of Malech, year 70 AG, day 5th. Narrow, winding trail in front of you, the dense woods on either side giving little to no vision. With the approaching sunset, long shadows of the trees dance across the ground. Trees, in fiery hues of autumn - brilliant oranges, reds and golds - move gently in a light breeze. The air is crisp and clear, filled with the earthy scent of dry leaves, and faint lingering scent of dried blood.

**Goblin encampment**

Players are investigating the peaceful parts of the Gray Forest, south of the village Holzenar, searching for a cunning goblin gang led by a hobgoblin. You have the option to divide the party, starting with at least 2 of the party members encountering goblin tracks on the road.

Venturing deeper into the woods, the trail leads them to a somewhat concealed goblin encampment. Encampment is divided into two distinct sections. In the first, around the fire, the hobgoblin leader, 4 goblins and a wolf are eating, drinking and laughing loudly. In the second area, surrounded by bushes, with just a narrow path accessing it, there is lone goblin with one wolf, guarding prisoners. Other players are here, chained and on the ground, alongside female Moravia Human, and male Rock Zenari.
Should the heroes opt for a stealthy approach or launch a surprise attack on the first area, the fight breaks out with lone goblin and wolf arriving in two rounds.
If they surprise or attack goblin in the second area, the goblin will run and try to get a backup, while the wolf will immediately attack the players. It takes two rounds for the goblins from the first section to reach the battle.

Two allies will try to release themselves as soon as the battle starts, even if the party decides not to help them. Once liberated, they join the combat on the side of the heroes.
Allies must succeed on a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check as they try to release themselves. If the players try to free them, they can use their attack or spell hit to shatter the chains. Every round DC becomes reduced by 2.

>Two characters, Zora Yakusheva of the Moravia humans and Ajaw Erakat, an Rock Zenari druid, are members of the Circle of Gaia. They have been investigating mysterious movements of evil forces and beasts within the forest, but their understanding of the events remains unclear. They believe someone in the nearby village may have deeper knowledge about the disturbances.

> Zora Yakusheva. A beautiful young woman dressed entirely in green. With fiery eyes and a strong attitude, she is a member of the Circle of Gaia. Determined to uncover the who is behind this unrest, Zora plans to contact other Circle members in the surrounding forests. She is prepared to pursue a quest and promises to return with information, if the party doesn't discover the Golden Hand first.

> Ajaw Erakat, A relentless protector, Ajaw is committed to ensuring Zora's safety. While he possesses deeper insights into the situation, he is not sharing them. Ajaw seeks a powerful artifact once belonging to the fallen Zenari Empire, intending to return it to its rightful place. He believes that allowing humans to wield this power would only lead to greater evil, therefore he is determined to prevent such an outcome.

**Village of Holzenar**

With the population of around 500, Holzenar is village in the south of Ragdar Kingdom. Village marshal is ex-soldier Alderac Schmit. Garrison includes around 30 guards and soldiers. Main professions are fishing, farming and woodcutting.

The party arrives at the village from its southern side, adjacent to the Woodcutter Lodge. Two woodcutters, a human and a dwarf, are busy stacking logs inside a small warehouse. They direct the party to a modest hut, which serves as the office of Boldar, a grumpy orc.

> The small cabin is just about spacious enough for this grumpy old orc to fit inside. Cabin is filled with a strong wooden scent, tainted with the stale stench of sweat. Even though there is one window inside, it seems it wasn't opened for some time. Although there is a window, it appears it hasn't been opened for some time. The cabin itself is modestly furnished with a single wooden chair and a table, with huge stack of papers spread on it. Orc is nose deep in the papers, tallying numbers, and paying no attention to anyone.
> Despite his age, he is a robust, muscular orc with broad shoulders.

Boldar Stonehoof, ex-soldier, is not fond of company and will not share any information about the movements of beasts and evil forces in the forest or the village, unless a character manages to persuade him.

The party can attempt to persuade and charm the old Orc into sharing the information.
With a proper roleplaying and a successful DC 15 Charisma (Persuasion) check, Boldar will reveal that something unusual is happening in both the forest and the village, but he is still unsure of the source. Afterwards, he will ask the party to leave.
With a successful DC 20 Charisma (Persuasion), he will additionally disclose to the party that he is losing his people in the forest, along with noticing strange behavior from both the beasts in the forest and the villagers. After this revelation, he will request the party to leave.
Should the party fail the check, Boldar will immediately demand they leave, becoming hostile and aggressive.

Entering the village, small paved road takes the players to the central square of the village.   You have freedom to create locals that can take players to play a game of dice, try to sell them fresh fish, or just point them to the tavern or town hall.
As there are many locations in Holzenar, you can suggest the players to start in the tavern.

**Tavern Drunken Pony**

>As you enter the tavern, the noise of chatting and eating fills your ears. The strong aroma of pork and beer greets you as soon as you step inside. An older Ragdar Human, with thinning dark hair, a beard, and a broad smile, manages the worn wooden bar. Eight tables are arranged around the room, four on each side. Beside the bar, a small wooden door likely leads to the kitchen, with a set of wooden stairs adjacent to it, ascending to the guest rooms. Although modest, the rooms are clean and equipped with hay beds.

The tavern is run by a chatty old barkeep named Pony, who also chose the tavern's name. He is an older Ragdar Human and has two sons who help manage the establishment—one as a cook and the other arranging boxes around the bar. There is only one barmaid, a dark-skinned woman who might be of Zargon origin, named Liannah.

Pony is eager to talk and shares with the party that unsettling changes have occurred in what was once a peaceful village. Since the beginning of the year, the village has experienced a series of mysterious events. The village marshal, a position typically held by ex-military personnel who oversee the local governance and security, has been unusually quiet for the past few weeks. Additionally, there has been an increase in the presence of Qi-Yian Humans, particularly around the village market. Reports of woodcutters and hunters going missing or being found dead have become frequent. Local wildlife has become increasingly active and aggressive. Moreover, unusual groups of bandits and goblins have been spotted wandering the Gray Forest.

Pony gives them locations around the city:
- Marshal is located in the Town Hall, in the northern part of the city, on the hill
- Market is just next to the city square, but it is closed during the night
- Woodcutter's Lodge is south of the city (if it wasn't already visited)

**Suspicious woodcutters**. Inside the inn at the moment are two figures sitting in the corner. Halfling and Moravia Human. They are woodcutters, and they are mourning over their friend who has been missing. They are sure they found his body three miles down south in the forest. Body was mauled, and when they went to pick it up a huge owlbear scared them and they ran for their lives. They are not fighters, they are simple woodcutters. They say that they will pay you well if you go and retrieve their friend, to give him a proper funeral.
They are 2 Golden Hand Scouts, and a contested insight check against their deception can read through their story. If the players realize they are lying they will try to evade the fight and run away. If captured, they will not talk.
They will also provide party with the map to the spot where they think their friend is (_Map is taking them to the traps in the Gray Forest, check Chapter 2_)

Their actual goal is to lead the party into the trap and kill them as they do not want anybody asking too much questions and possibly spoiling their plans. They are working for Edenezer Gozzra, leader of the Golden Hand mercenaries. They are employed to create distractions on the south border of Ragdar by scaring people away, sending bandit and goblin parties, and chasing them into the Gray forest.

**Market**

> The scent of fresh fish spreads through the village square. Small shops crowd together, forming a bustling bazaar filled with people buying fish, fruits, and vegetables. In a small shop on the corner, an old lady sells flowers. At the center of the market, a loud man energetically tries to sell the day's catch of fish. Nearby, a sweet old halfling lady sells apples at a low price, hoping to earn enough for today's food.

Although the market is organized like a bazaar with many small shops, it operates under a central administration since vendors must rent space to sell their goods. Surya Patel, a Qi-Yian human, oversees this market. She is a member of the Tajir Guild, known for her short temper and tendency to yell at her employees. Dressed in an orange and purple silk suit, styled more traditionally for men, she is an older woman who is known to be harsh but fair in her dealings. She informs the party that there are mysterious figures in golden armor in the forest, though she knows neither their identities nor their intentions.

**Lost caravan**. Surya is troubled by missing shipments that were supposed to arrive from the north, from Ulfheim. She fears something has happened to their caravan. She offers 50 gold per party member if they can recover at least half of the lost goods. The last caravan was transporting valuable jewelry. If the party successfully completes this quest, she will offer them membership in the Tajir Guild.

**Town hall**

>Heading north, near the northeast gate, you can spot a hill with two small watchtowers and a path leading to a two-story stone building featuring a large wooden entrance door. Guards at the entrance will allow you to enter to speak with the marshal but will escort you throughout your visit. Inside, a long hall leads to a small table where a clerk records your names before granting further access. The walls of the hall are decorated with portraits of what you assume are famous generals and warriors in the history of the Ragdar Kingdom. Once permitted, you ascend the stairs to the side and enter a spacious, well-lit, and warm room, filled with the smell of cooked meat and fine wine. The marshal is in the middle of his lunch at a central table, which has two chairs facing it. Bookshelves line both sides of the room, and a large map of the Ragdar Kingdom hangs on the wall behind the marshal.

Marshal Alderac Schmit is an aging man who relies on a cane to walk. He wears a red vest over padded armor that still fits his strong, muscular body. He gives the party a narrative that nothing unusual is happening, explaining that it's common for people to migrate north before the end of autumn due to the harsh winters that disrupt woodworking and fishing. However, even if the party suspects he is withholding information, he refuses to reveal the true situation.
**Dryad lair**. Marshal Schmit indicates his need for adventurers. His scouts have reported that west of the village lies a dryad's lair. The dryad has been using wasps and other insects to harass local fishermen and hunters, threatening the area's food supply. Additionally, there has been a recent fatality attributed to the dryad. Marshal Schmit tasks the party with resolving this threat—either by eliminating the dryad or convincing her to leave—and offers a reward of 150 gold.

**Ready for the Gray Forest**

After tending to matters around the village, evening approaches, and the players should seek a place to sleep. With the Beef and Ale closed for a few days, they head to the Drunken Pony. In the morning, after they have rested, Zora is waiting for them downstairs in the tavern.

> The troubled expression on young Zora's face worries you. She is eagerly awaiting your arrival. The tavern is already partially filled with guests, having their breakfast and morning drink before heading off to work.

She informs the party that their elder, guide, and one of the most important people in the Circle of Gaia is also missing. Zora and Ajaw plan to search for him in the forest. She describes him as a tall Moravia Human, dressed in earth tones dominated by green, with brown hair and a beard. She advises the party to investigate what is happening in the Gray Forest, emphasizing that it is an urgent matter, before she leaves.

_For the following part, the Raven Syndicate representative could be a family member or relative of one of the characters, or you could simply approach the player whose character doesn’t adhere strictly to a moral code._

In a corner table, a Human and an Ocean Zenari sit, closely watching the party. One of the party members hears a message in their head: "Meet us at the back." If the player decides to separate from the party and goes to the back of the tavern, the following hook unfolds. However, if the player informs the party or tells them about the message, no one will be waiting at the back of the tavern.

**Raven Syndicate offer.** The Human named Arvid and the Ocean Zenari are part of the Raven Syndicate, a global organization specializing in information mastery. They ask the player to steal a green emerald stone from Zora Yakusheva. They offer the player 100 gold and membership in the Raven Syndicate as a reward. Arvid is unsure of the stone's purpose but has been instructed to retrieve it, offering a potential new recruit a chance to prove themselves. After presenting their proposition, the Ocean Zenari mage casts Invisibility, and they both vanish without a trace.

Before leaving the village, advance players to level 2.
