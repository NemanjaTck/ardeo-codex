**Leaving the Faorburd**

Upon exiting the chapel, you find yourself back in the forest. From this vantage point, you can see Faorburd in the distance and a small trail leading further northeast. A wisp of smoke is visible in the distance beyond the forest trees. After an hour of walking, the outline of a roadside inn comes clearly into view.

**Roadside Inn** "Meat and Ale"

>Leaving the dirt forest trail, you come upon what resembles a road. Made of unevenly laid bricks, it leads to a small wooden inn. Upon arrival, you notice seven horses tied to a hitching rail and an empty cart off to the side. Four black horses, each with a black saddle, are all tied together on one side.

If the character on the Raven Syndicate quest managed to steal the emerald from Zora, the following event unfolds: The character with the highest passive perception will notice a large bag tied across the saddle of the first horse. Long brown hair protrudes from it, and upon closer inspection, a face can be recognized—it is the dead body of Zora Yakusheva, tied and strapped in a bag. If the players manage to interrogate any Raven Syndicate members inside the inn, they will confess that they fought and heavily wounded a druid while killing a ranger. They also reveal that ranger and druid killed three of their own comrades. Arvid, a high-ranking officer, had given them the emerald, which aided in locating their targets.

>The heavy wooden door at the entrance is heavily damaged, showing signs of forced entry with pieces of wood glued back together and some sections completely missing. A potent stench of various meats mixed with the strong aroma of cheap ale hits you as you enter. Inside, ten tables are scattered around the room. As is typical for inns, the bar is directly in front of you. Some tables are occupied, while others remain empty for the moment. In one corner, a young group of Zenari travelers is drinking and singing, and a small Halfling family is quietly enjoying their dinner. On the opposite side, a very pretty young Human woman sits with an older elf.

(This is Leena Desai, who was waiting to see if the party would survive her challenge). They quickly get up and leave; if confronted, they will use Misty Step followed by Invisibility to escape.

>Next to their table sits a group of four Raven Syndicate members, their sigils displayed openly. They are drinking and laughing, ignoring others around them.

>Behind the small wooden bar is an old Dwarf named Rodon Dawood. The scars on his face suggest that this place can see its fair share of violence. With a smile full of broken and missing teeth, he welcomes the travelers, “Hello and welcome to the Meat and Ale. The choice of meals and drinks is limited, but we can make sure you are not hungry or thirsty. We also have some rooms available.”

>The rooms are located in a hall behind the bar, which emits a strong odor, and the occasional rat scurries across the floor. Each room contains two straw beds covered with dirty linen. The doors are damaged and can barely close, offering no possibility of locking.

If asked, Rodon will share information about the inn and the nearby gold mine. He tells the party that his inn has stood for a long time, surviving encounters with various beasts and bandits. Despite its remote location, with no village or city nearby, it frequently hosts travelers. He mentions smaller outposts in the iron mines deeper in the mountains and a deserted gold mine. Rodon, who rarely leaves his property, has heard tales of an artifact within the mine, but no one who has sought it has returned to confirm its existence. Many groups that have passed through his inn were lost in that deadly mine. He explains that the mine is easily accessible, just a few hours' walk along the path leading further into the mountains.

Following Rodon's directions, the players quickly reach the massive entrance of the gold mine. The exterior is cluttered with broken wooden platforms surrounding the entrance, mining carts lying on their sides, and bags of unused food scattered about. A small stone path leads into the opening of the mine.

**Gold Mine**

This was once one of the greatest mines in the Ragdar Kingdom. Following a series of inexplicable events, the mine was shut down and left abandoned. The Ragdar Kingdom retains ownership of the mine, and a decree prohibits anyone from using the mine and its halls for their own purposes.

**Ceiling**. The stone ceiling varies in height from 8 to 15 feet, depending on which part of the mine you are in.

**Walls**. The stone walls on the sides are still in good condition. In many places, they are supported by wooden arches or various wooden constructions reinforced with iron.

**Light**. The largest part of the mine is in complete darkness. Some rooms that are currently inhabited may have light, based on the description provided for each specific area.

1. Mine platform

>As you enter the wide mine entrance, you are greeted by a hall that stretches for 60 feet. The light gradually fades, and the songs of birds and the clash of winds grow distant. The air is heavy with the stench of rotten corpses, making it difficult to breathe. Eventually, you arrive at the edge. In front of you lies a small wooden elevator, seemingly out of function.

A player with darkvision can discern that the ground beneath is just 3 feet away, indicating that the elevator was probably used for transporting gold.

2. Mine entry hall

>Stepping down to the bottom part, you find yourself at a crossroads. The rotten stench of decomposing bodies mixes with a high level of moisture in the air. You can hear the sound of bones crackling somewhere in one of the tunnels. Two torches, emitting magical light, decorate the walls. Directly ahead, you see a door framed with a stone arch, precisely carved with runes and symbols. To the left and right, tunnels stretch out, descending slightly as they delve deeper into the mine.

3. Dungeon tunnels

>The sound of bones crackling grows louder as you venture deeper into the dungeon. Soon, the entry hall is no longer visible behind you. The walls here are splattered with dried blood, and bones are strewn alongside small marbles and stones chipped from the walls. The hall darkens progressively as the light from the entry hall fades.

From the tunnels on the left, players are directed towards the prison cells. Ask the players for a group DC 10 Dexterity (Stealth) check. If they fail as a group, or if they make a right turn before reaching the cells, they will encounter a Skeleton Minotaur that attacks the group.

4. Jail cells

>You finally discover the source of the bone crackling sound. In two small cells, barred with iron and seemingly locked, you see eight skeletons, packed four to each cell. Scattered around them are small pouches, clothes, and some broken weapons. Though they look hostile, they do not attack.

The skeletons will shout, "Free us!" They will not initiate an attack on the players first. Only four of them are equipped with a shortbow. If the players decide to release them, the skeletons will immediately attack.

**Treasure**: In the first cell, one pouch is empty, but another contains 3 jewels worth 15 gold each and 1d4 platinum coins. Thieves' tools can also be found lying on the ground in the second cell.

5. Drain room

>As you navigate through the dark tunnels, a quick glimpse of running water catches your eye somewhere ahead. You enter a larger part of the cave, where the ceiling is much higher than in the tunnels. Two torches emitting magical light provide a clear view of the room, which features a large drain in the center. You notice a heavy metal shaft with iron bars positioned over a circular hole, presumably used for draining waste.

Upon entering the room, ask the players for a group DC 13 Dexterity (Stealth) check. If they fail as a group, 9 sleeping stirges attached to the ceiling will awaken and attack. If the players look up and spot the stirges, grant them a surprise round over the stirges. On the wall south of the shaft, there are two levers: one is pulled down, while the other is up. The lever that's pulled down controls the opening and closing of the shaft in this room. The other lever controls the shaft in room 12.

6. Assayers office

>Exiting the hall from the drain room, you encounter a small wooden door that barely stands. A slight push opens the doors wide, revealing a small assayer's office. The smell of old, rotten wood dominates, overtaking the moisture in the air. On a small table, there is a set of scales and scattered papers. Two shelves on the sides hold massive books filled with weigh-in reports. Behind the chair at the end of the room, there is a small wooden safe.

**Treasure**: The table holds many documents, some dating back 100 years. The wooden safe, which is unlocked, contains 2d10 gold pieces, 2d20 silver pieces, 1d100 copper pieces, and a scroll of Identify.

7. Crypt

>As you approach the open stone arch, the chilling sound of bone gnawing terrifies you. You hear multiple creatures feasting on corpses, and the stench of rot and decay fills the air. Entering the crypt, you realize that it was used for services held for deceased miners whose bodies were buried here. Ghouls, busy with feeding on the bones, immediately turn their heads towards you and launch an attack.

In this room, 4 ghouls are feeding on the remains of dead miners. All the caskets in the crypt are open, and the bodies have been disturbed. An additional 4 ghouls will join the fight from the nearby hall upon hearing the commotion.

**Treasure**: Two potions of healing can be found inside one of the caskets.

8. Main tunnels

If the players haven't yet encountered ghouls, read:

>You hear footsteps approaching from both sides of the tunnel. The disgusting sound of gnawing on bones echoes through the tunnel, growing louder and more rapid.

4 ghouls emerge from the darkness and attack the players. Another 4 ghouls from the nearby crypt will join the fray upon hearing the fight. Amidst the rubble on the side, the player with the highest passive perception notices a small, worn-out leather diary. The diary contains many runes and texts in the Zenari language. If there is a party member who can read Zenari, they decipher the last few pages where Run'Garad expresses his fear of being followed and watched.

9. Dining room

>As you approach the well-lit room, a thudding sound, like a rock clashing against another, grows louder. Something is moving, but it's unclear what. The smell of mold and earth intensifies with each step. A huge stone arch frames the entrance to what appears to be a dining hall. Tables are neatly arranged along the sides, and a bar stands at the far end of the room. The thudding sound becomes louder as rock formations on the ceiling begin to shift and move, revealing an Earth Elemental emerging from them.

The Earth Elemental attacks the players on sight, serving as a guardian of this room, which is used by Run'Garad. Food in the room is well-preserved in barrels, and the bar is well-stocked with drinks. Tables and silverware are clean and organized.

**Treasure**: On one of the tables, you find Rouge Bracers.

10. Crystal cavern

>The noises from other parts of the mine fade away, leaving everything deadly silent. The smell of moisture mixed with the rotten stench dissipates. You enter a cave decorated with crystals on the ceiling and walls, which emit a bright light throughout the cavern. At the other end, there is a crate.

As players attempt to move across the cavern, they must succeed on a DC 13 Dexterity (Stealth) check every 10 feet to avoid dislodging the crystals. If the crystals fall, the player underneath must make a DC 11 Dexterity saving throw. On a roll of 1-4, they receive 2d8 piercing damage; on a roll of 5-10, they receive only 1d8.

**Treasure**: The crate holds a greater potion of healing.

11. Shaft tunnels

>A strong smell of fungus mixed with moisture pervades the tunnel to the right. The odor is so heavy that you can almost visualize a green trail of it leading downward. As you descend through the tunnels, you notice an opening ahead, with green smoke emanating from it.

12. Flooded shaft

>As you arrive, you nearly lose your footing, stepping into a pool of green liquid that fills the entire room. This translucent, smelly liquid covers the floor up to the level of the stairs you are standing on. Across the room, you can see another set of stairs and a tunnel continuing in that direction.

Players need to navigate through the poisonous water carefully. Beneath the liquid, there is a closed shaft. The walls in the room are steep and offer no handholds. The lever in room 5 opens this shaft and allows the liquid to drain away. Any contact with the liquid inflicts 2d8 poison damage on the creature and poisons them for the next hour.

13. Shrine to Vazeer

>As you approach the doors, the iron smell of blood fills your nostrils, and you feel a strange energy emanating from within the room. Upon opening the door, you see a pool of blood directly in front of you. At the back of the room stands a marble statue.

This is a shrine dedicated to Vazeer, the God of torture and blood. Bathing in the pool of blood grants players a +2 bonus on Constitution Saving Throws for the next hour.

14. Storage room

>Opening the door to a small storage room, you find that the room is set in a dugout hole. A small ladder allows you to step down into this 6 foot deep space. Old barrels topped with food and some dusty chests stored at the back suggest that this room has been used recently.

This storage room is actively used by Run'Garad for items he doesn't frequently need.

**Treasure**: Among the old, dusty chests, you can find 2 potions of Vitality and a Cloak of Arachnida.

15. Library

>You enter a well-lit room with a tiled floor, surrounded by shelves brimming with books that are meticulously dust-free. Several books have been removed from their places and lie scattered across the desks around the room. in this well-equipped library, books are covering topics such as history, arcana, and religion.

Library is guarded by a Revenant that attacks on sight.

**Treasure**: Among the desks scattered around the room, players can find scrolls of Nystul's Magic Aura and Invisibility.

16. Alchemist lab

>Across from the library, a small hall leads to well-decorated and designed wooden doors. These doors are unlocked, and as you slowly enter, you find yourself in a space that serves both as a laboratory and living quarters. The strong smell of herbs and potions fills the air, while bright lights emanate from various colored bottles around the room. Herbs and vines grow from the roof. You see two vampire spawns, one sitting on a bed and the other on a chair.

The vampires are not immediately hostile and seek explanations from the group. If they sense hostility or detect a lie during the conversation, they will attack immediately. If the group manages to convince them that they mean no harm to their master, Run'Garad, the vampires will allow the group to leave the room while they remain on guard.

**Treasure**: If the group manages to kill or deceive the vampires, the only treasure in the room is a fully brewed Potion of Haste.

17. The Fountain

>As you navigate the hall, a huge stone archway comes into view. Entering this small courtyard with a stone ceiling supported by four pillars, you notice a beautiful white marble fountain with running water. The pretty lady you previously saw in the tavern is sitting on the edge of the fountain. Two warriors in black armor stand close by her, and there is an unconscious body of an old Zenari lying in front of them.

Leena greets the players warmly, expressing relief that they managed to arrive in one piece. After a brief conversation, she delivers her speech and presents the players with options.

**Leena's Speech**: "From the days before the first kingdoms, when Ishhalor breathed life into the world, Elves regarded every other being as beneath them, ruling as overlords. For too long, they exploited Zenari, Humans, Dwarves, and other races in their mines, enriching themselves. Yes, now anyone can live in Elven cities, but you will always be seen as lesser beings to them. My cause is noble: to forge a fresh start, where every soul is treated as equal." At this point, she speaks mostly truth, a bit exaggerated. A good Insight check will confirm her sincerity. "The gods don’t care about us—they never have. By opening this gate, we will make them take notice. You have three choices: You can leave, letting me alone be remembered in the annals of history. You can try to fight me, which will not end well for you. Or, you can join me in a grand endeavor, an event that will change the world forever. So, what will it be?" She genuinely believes that by opening the Abyss gate, she will free Inis, who will grant her immense power and make her her first general.

Leena is a level 7 wizard, accompanied by two Golden Hand Soldiers.

**Campaign Conclusion**: The choice the players make here could serve as the starting hook for a new campaign where they must face the consequences of their actions or encounter others trying to open the gates. This pivotal moment offers them a chance to influence the direction of their adventure and the world around them.
