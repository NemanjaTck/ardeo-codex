**Arrival to Holzenar**

Waiting in the Drunken Pony is Ellie Stoutfoot, owner of the Remedy shop. She begins by discussing an artifact from the Zenari Empire, rumored to be hidden in an abandoned gold mine northeast of the village. Legend has it that this artifact was used to open the gates to the Abyss realm and summon demons. It was during the opening of these gates that the united forces of Elves, Humans, Dwarves, and Orcs, collectively known as the armies of Amalgam, joined with the Zenari Empire to repel the demon hordes. The old wizard is now seeking this artifact, though his motives remain unclear.
The road to the mine leads through the abandoned village of Faorburd, which was depopulated during the War of the Elements. Since then, no one has returned, as there are tales of lost souls still haunting the village streets.
Ellie may also introduce her personal quest for the sage from Chapter 1 if the party has not already met her. After her departure, Zora and Ajaw will also leave, heading north to Ulfheim to alert one of the Ragdar kings about the current events.

The character who undertook the quest for the emerald will receive a telepathic message to meet Arvid and the Ocean Zenari at the northern gate at sundown. If the character has successfully obtained the emerald and hands it over to the Raven Syndicate members, they will be granted membership in the Syndicate, receive a versatile badge, and earn 50 gold as promised.

After leaving the tavern, a friend of one of the players will approach them, possibly in the city square or another location. This friend will reveal that Leena Desai, one of the three heads of the Qi-Yian Trade League, is implicated in the unfolding events. The friend will suggest that the group leave the village and steer clear of the artifact quest. However, recognizing the party’s resolve to continue, the friend will offer one last tip: search for the map to the mine in the old chapel in Faorburd.

**Side quest: North for missing caravan**

> Heading north of the village, you follow a small dirt trail that gradually widens into a more defined road as you are closer to Ulfheim. Along the way, you encounter other travelers, some riding, others walking, carrying boxes and crates on small wheelbarrows and carriages. After an hour’s walk, a hill looms behind a small forest in the distance.

Two hours into your journey and following the road, you come upon the remains of a caravan at the entrance to the small forest.

>You arrive at a scene of chaos: the caravan is overturned, crates are scattered about, and trails of blood lead into the caravan. Inside, you discover the bodies of three individuals, presumably guards, packed into the caravan. The crates are nearly empty, save for a few pieces of jewelry.

Exploring around this clearing, or with a successful DC 10 Wisdom (Survival) check, players can find tracks leading northwest through the forest. After just a few minutes, the party arrives at a clearing in front of a cave, where the aftermath of a battle is evident. You see four bodies on the ground, their cloaks torn and bodies mauled and scratched. A successful DC 12 Intelligence (Investigation) check reveals Raven Syndicate badges on the bodies. With a successful DC 15 Intelligence (Nature) check, a player can deduce that the marks on the bodies are from an owlbear, noting the scratches and beak bites. Approaching the cave, you notice a wounded owlbear sleeping, with two small cubs playfully interacting with the crates. The cave is relatively small with a large opening. It appears that bandits from the Raven Syndicate intended to use the cave to stash the crates until they could organize them for transport, but they chose the wrong cave. If a fight breaks out, the owlbear uses a normal stat block but with just 50% of its HP, while its cubs have a third of its health and deal a third of its damage.

Treasure: You find four crates filled with jewelry, with a market value of over 1000 gold. If the players are unaware, you should inform them that stealing or potentially reselling the jewelry could have consequences.

**Side quest: The Old Sage**

>Heading into the small woods west of Holzenar, nestled between two smaller hills, you follow the given directions and soon arrive at a small cabin. The fire pit in front of the entrance is still warm. Nearby, a clothes rack bears some rags tossed over it, and a small pot containing an unfinished meal, possibly mushrooms with some leaves, sits beside it.

When the players enter the cabin, read:

>The small wooden cabin, standing just about 7 feet tall, comprises only one modest room. As you enter, the smell of tea leaves and incense mixed with a stale stench greets you. Old, crooked wooden shelves, burdened with more books than they seem capable of holding, line the walls. A small coffee table, with a cup and a teapot, and a single chair occupy the space in the middle of the cabin. Instead of a bed, a simple, gray, dirty bedroll occupies a corner of the room.

If the players arrive during the night, the sage will surprise them by returning from his bedtime meditation. If they arrive during the day, just northeast of the cabin, in a small clearing within the forest, the old sage can be found sitting and meditating.

>Clad in old gray robes and leaning heavily on his wooden staff, this elderly, gray-haired human, with a beard that reaches longer than his waist, does not give off the impression of a wise sage with the ability to discern all the mysteries of the world around him.

The sage welcomes the players warmly, offering to make them tea and proving to be friendly and communicative. He explains that he chose this reclusive life to escape the obligations of modern society, finding companionship only with animals and freedom in the surrounding forest.

If the players attempt to extract information from him, he informs them that they must first pass his test by solving his riddles. Only after they correctly answer all three will he divulge the information they seek. He also cautions that once they have succeeded or failed, they are forbidden from seeking him out again.

_If the players decide to return after completing or failing the riddles, they will find his cabin, but they will never again find or see him._

The riddles are in order
1. What two things can you never eat for breakfast?
   Lunch and dinner
2. An airship crashed between Zargon Republic and Tsardom of Moravia. Where were the survivors buried?
   They weren’t. Survivors don’t need to be buried.
3. The more of this there is, the less you see. What is it?
   Darkness

You may choose to use these riddles or substitute your own. If the players correctly guess all three, the sage will provide a truthful answer to one of their questions.

**Faorburd - the Ruined Chapel**

PHOTO

> You enter an old and ruined chapel, with pillars that barely support the roof. Two sides of the building are almost completely exposed. At the far end of the chapel, a dusty altar stands, with a set of stairs to the side.

Ground floor of the chapel holds nothing. Soft glimmer of light can be seen coming from the stairs leading down into the basement.

1. Chapel Basement

>You descend into the chapel basement. The basement is creepy silent but well-lit, with torches that seem to emit a magical light, illuminating the room much more brightly than ordinary torches would. You see pillars supporting the upper floor and some crates tossed aside next to the heavy stone walls. At the far end of the room, you notice a mirror about 7 feet tall and 5 feet wide.

Upon looking through the mirror, players realize that it displays the same room, but filled with gold. The mirror is actually a portal.

2. Mirrored Room

Read as players go through the portal:

>You enter a room that almost mirrors the previous one. You see a lectern and two chests at the end of the room. The gold that was visible before is now absent. You feel something in the air. Something is not quite right.

The moment all players go through the portal read this:

>You feel a sharp sting. Your breath becomes heavy, and vision blurs for a brief moment. When your sight clears, you realize you are not in the same spot as before, you have teleported a short distance. Looking at your hands, you notice they are not your own. Your clothes, your hair, everything is different.

At this moment, you switch the players' characters, exchanging their character sheets. Each player retains their consciousness and memory, but now possesses the physical characteristics and knowledge of the body they inhabit.

When they approach lectern read:

>On the marble lectern rests an open book. Its pages are old, wrinkled, and torn, but the text is written in perfect black ink: "Welcome, I have been expecting you. It would be too easy and boring to simply eliminate you, given the potential within your group. Let’s put that to test. Open the chests on both sides; I've left something you'll definitely need if you wish to exit the chapel. When you are ready, turn the page. Good luck. —Eneelaidas."

When they turn the page a small, hidden stone door opens to the left.

Treasure: Players gain equipment to help them get through the dungeon.
Poison Longbow - Longbow +1, deals additional 2 poison damage on hit.
Posion Dagger - Dagger +1, deals additional 2 poison damage on hit.
Bronze shield - Shield +1, additional +3 AC.
Storm ring - Ring with spell hit + 1,  one additional thunder damage on spell hit.

3. Blood Wall

>You enter a small square room, enclosed by stone walls on each side with only the entrance as an opening. The room is completely dark.

As the players approach the northern wall with a light source or if a character has darkvision, they discover writing on the wall in black paint: "Offer yourself, paint me red, but beware, what you offer, will come back, and give you a scare."

The players need to paint the wall with blood. Whatever they use—be it their own blood, that of a person, or a beast—a clone made of blood will manifest behind them and initiate a battle. After they defeat this blood clone, the northern wall retracts, leading the players into a hallway.

4. Hall

>As you enter the hall, you smell the strange scent of incense, almost perfumed, and immediately notice the opposite wall, with two burning torches and an unlit candle situated between them. To your right, you see a broken fountain and, behind it, door frames to both the left and right. At the end of this well-lit hall stands an open door frame, without any door.

Pulling the candle reveals a hidden passage leading to the interrogation room. If the party reaches the end of the hall, read:

>You approach the frame, and about 10 feet in front, you begin to see your reflection as if in a mirror. Beside the passage, a word is inscribed on a plaque attached to the wall: "Kindness."

The passage is under a spell. Whatever actions the characters take, their reflections mimic them perfectly. There is no glass or any physical barrier between them; they can even feel the touch of their reflections. To solve the riddle and open the passage, players need to allow their reflections to pass through first. Any gesture of politeness, such as a simple bow, a hand gesture, or saying “after you,” will resolve the riddle.

5. Interrogation Room

>As you enter this hidden room, you find a large stretcher at the center, between a work table with various tools on one side and a cage on the other. The room appears clean, but a faint scent of dried blood reveals its true purpose.

Behind the work table stands a set of magnificent ornate double doors, decorated with a small emblem. With a successful DC 16 Intelligence (History) check, a player can identify that the emblem is a symbol of Inis, traditionally used on the banners of the Inis Protectorate.
If the players make any loud noises or disturb any tools located on the table, two ghosts apparate from the walls, and a fight breaks out. The ornate double doors are protected by a magical trap. With a successful DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check, players can detect a small string that, when pushed, activates two holes on both walls flanking the doors. Players standing next to the door when the trap is triggered take 2d6 fire damage. The doors themselves are not magical and can be opened with thieves’ tools and a successful DC 15 Dexterity check, or they can be knocked down with a successful DC 25 Strength check.

6. Office

>As you step into the room, lit by softly glowing orbs that hover near the ceiling, a sweet, almost intoxicating aroma of rare herbs and spices greets you. In the center of the room, a large wooden table is strewn with ancient scrolls and detailed maps. Among the papers, there are different documents and analysis of Ragdar Kingdom and its defences, list of high ranking military officers, data on both of the capitals. To each side of the room stand towering bookshelves, filled with tomes bound in leather. In one far corner of the room lies a well-made bed, its linens fine and smooth, suggesting comfort. The opposite corner holds a pretty, ornately decorated safe.

This room served as an office of Leena Desai.

Treasure: At the table, players can find Scroll of blindness/deafness and a bag with 1d4 platinum. Safe is open, and inside there is fine clothes, valued at 25 gold pieces. One document is almost destroyed. Only a few words could be discerned from it - "Artifact", "Open", "Abyss plane".

7. Praying chamber

>The wooden door leading to this room is unlocked. Upon entering, you hear a soft, gentle female voice say, “What you cannot see, cannot hurt you.” Your gaze immediately shifts to a statue of a female entity at the far end of the room. Broken stone benches and damaged stone pillars supporting the roof suggest that this room was once used for prayers. After a few initial seconds, you hear drums in the distance. Then silence once again.

The more time players spend in the room, the shorter the silence between the drumbeats becomes, and the drum sound grows louder. After a few initial minutes, two basilisks emerge from holes on both sides of the statue, attacking the players.

With a successful DC 15 Intelligence (History) check, players can recognize that the statue is of the goddess Neiri, and that people actually prayed here in front of the statue. Any player who prays and kneels in front of Neiri receives Neiri’s blessing: darkvision up to 60 feet and advantage on all saving throws against spells that can charm, stun, paralyze, petrify, or frighten until the next long rest.

8. Mausoleum

> The wooden door to this room is also unlocked. You enter the small mausoleum, which is without any light source and shrouded in complete darkness. The only visible light comes from a dim glow on three small plaques.

As you approach with a light or if players have darkvision, they can discern three stone sarcophagi in the room. Attached to each is a plaque glowing with dim light, reading in order: “Here lies a knight,” “Here lies a thief,” and “Here lies a fool.” A small inscription in front of the three sarcophagi states:

>In front of you are a knight, a thief, and a fool. You want to open the sarcophagus of the knight, which will reward you, while the other two will definitely punish you. You can ask only one question, to only one sarcophagus, and you will receive a response. Good luck.

The challenge is designed to be unsolvable. The correct order is inscribed on the sides of the sarcophagi - (Thief, Fool, Knight - top to bottom).

Treasure: The knight’s sarcophagus contains one greater potion of healing, a potion of speed, 1d4 gold coins, and 2d10 silver coins. Opening the fool’s sarcophagus forces the player to laugh uncontrollably for the next 30 minutes, with effects similar to being poisoned. Opening the thief’s sarcophagus magically removes all the coins earned throughout the dungeon.

9. Hallway

>By showing kindness you manage to pass through the magical trap, into the dark and freezing hallway. Your attention is immediately drawn to your left, where you can see multiple eyes watching you from darkness.

There are 1 spectator and 2 gazers in this room that attack players on sight.

Written on the wall in blood are the instructions: “Go left, jump on one leg, avoiding all metal floor tiles.” The floor on the left is arranged with alternating simple black and white tiles and heavy metal tiles. The right side displays trails of blood on the walls and scattered skeleton parts throughout the small hall.

By pulling the lever, players inadvertently open the bars to the north, releasing two minotaurs that charge towards the players and attack.
