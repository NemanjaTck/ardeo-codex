Founded in 1541 BG, during the migration of the Elves, Nohenjo Daro initially emerged as the first Elf-settled city. Today, it stands as the first human city and the capital of Zargon, renowned for its expansive libraries and academies, rivaling only those in Solarion. Zargon cities are distinguished by their ziggurats—massive temples that serve as centers for religion and research.

#### Population

40,000 (55% Zargon human, 25% elves, 10% other human races, 10% other races)

#### Government

Zargon operates as a meritocracy, where intelligence and wisdom dictate leadership. Each small settlement is overseen by a magister appointed by the Council, while Nohenjo Daro is governed by High Magister Ermund Zad the Second, who presides in the High Ziggurat of Nohenjo Daro.

#### City defenses

Instead of a standing army, each city in Zargon maintains its own Kadir Vanguard unit, named after the Goddess of desert and sand. This unit comprises cavalry riders and archers using camels and horses for desert traversal. Within city walls, they serve as infantry and archers. There is also an elite unit known for taming and riding Sand Wyrms, formidable desert beasts. The city’s fortifications include robust walls and towers equipped with high-precision ballistas.

#### Organizations

Nohenjo Daro serves as the headquarters of the Academia, attracting scholars, alchemists, wizards, and academics eager to advance their research. The Leari Order and Greatwyrm Scribes have a significant presence, integrating into the city’s scholarly and defense activities.

#### Architecture

Utilizing the Lumis River that flows through it, Nohenjo Daro has engineered numerous small waterfalls, enhancing the city’s beauty and supporting river life. The city is interconnected by five bridges.

**High Ziggurat**: Standing as the city’s most prominent structure, this 450-foot tall ziggurat is adorned with waterfalls and gardens, making it one of the tallest buildings in the world.

**Zargon Skyport**: Nohenjo Daro houses the largest skyport on the Westglade continent, facilitating extensive air travel and trade.

**Great Library**: Biggest library in the world, that gathers massive amount of writings from different historians and different eras, wizard teachings and research papers.

#### Resources

Nohenjo Daro thrives on a rich array of magic shops, libraries, and academies found throughout the city. Its location on the fertile banks of the Lumis River bolsters agriculture, which is a primary source of food and income. River docks and caravan routes extend trade to the continent of Eurenis and southward to Zenari cities.

#### Lore

Nohenjo Daro is unique in its history as the only city that was never conquered or witnessed war within its walls. It has long been a sanctuary for travelers, whose contributions have enriched the city’s libraries and archives. These institutions, alongside academies for arcane studies, have established Nohenjo Daro as a pivotal center of research, culture, and religion on the Westglade continent. During the War of the Elements, Nohenjo Daro repelled all attacks, leading to the establishment of a national holiday, the Day of Victory, commemorating the decisive defeat of Zenari Roc riders and airships by Zargon ballistas and mages, with the Kadir Vanguard driving the remaining ground forces back into the Luvani desert.
