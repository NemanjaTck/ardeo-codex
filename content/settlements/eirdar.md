People of Eldrar and Bjornblod that were tired of constant fighting, both internally and with Ragdar Kingdom, set course for the distant island in the east. Soon they found themselves on the island of Hvitil, and founded the city of Eirdar in the lagoon of Viktor bay. Founded in 1077 BG, Eirdar even today stands as a sanctuary for all people looking for peace.

#### Population

14,400 (75% Dwarves, 10% Humans, 5% Gnomes, 10% other races)

#### Government

Eirdar is a capital city and the residence of the Hammarborg clan. Like every other Dwarven city, Eirdar doesn’t have a ruler, but caretaker, in the role of chieftain. Every family has one representative in the political structures of the city. City has the People's Council for daily and local activities, and the High Council for more important things like foreign relations, defense and other crucial stuff. Current chieftain is Davadar “the Gray” Hammarborg.

#### City defenses

Hammarborg clan has a standing army, residing in Eirdar. Higher army personnel and experienced soldiers serve as city guard. Every adult male and female of the clan is obligated to report to military camp in Eirdar, once a year, for a one week training, to stay in shape in case of an emergency threat for the city.

#### Organizations

Tajir Guild is present in the city and takes part in a lot of market and finance activities. Greatwyrm Scribes have their offices, as well as the Circle of Gaia, scouting and exploring surrounding mountains. Outside of a few thieves guilds, criminal activity is pretty low inside the city, with no major criminal organizations or black markets.

#### Architecture

People of the clan Hammarborg have devoted their lives living in peace, worship of the god Koval, and mining and blacksmithing as professions.

**Underground tunnels**: City of Eirdar is connected to the mines in the Magadur mountains with a series of underground tunnels. Tunnel network is very well developed, but with lacking security, allowing thieves to use it for smuggling goods

**Great Forge**: Sprawling in the northern part of the city, the massive forge building is a birthplace of many weapons, armors, shields and other equipment. Best smiths of the city are working in the Great Forge, refining and using both ores mined in Magadur mines, and the ones traded from around the world.

#### Resources

The bustling mines in the Magadur mountains are the primary occupation, production, and export source for the city of Eirdar. The docks are bustling with traders and barges that transport ore and refined metals to other cities and continents. Besides metals, the city exports various other goods, including furs and pearls. Additionally, there is a small salt refinery north of the city.

#### Lore

Since its founding, the Hammarborg clan has led a peaceful existence. Their standing army has traditionally been tasked with peacekeeping duties, and for nearly a thousand years, Hammarborg did not engage in any conflicts. However, when the War of the Elements erupted, Hammarborg, alongside the Stormson clan, heeded the elves' call for aid. They mobilized over 50 warships under Admiral Enge Fireborn, aiming to reach Galadhal as swiftly as possible. Admiral Fireborn survived the war and returned to Eirdar hailed as a hero.
