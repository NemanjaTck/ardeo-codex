In the heart of the jungle, along the K'uhad River and adjacent to what was once the North Stjarna Sea, now an ocean, stands the massive and second oldest city of the Zenari, Aghanat. It is the largest Zenari port and one of the most significant trading hubs in the Ardeo world.

#### Population

37,000 (45% Ocean Zenari, 25% other Zenari, 10% Humans, 10% Elves, 10% other races)

#### Government

The Mayek tribe and the state of Mayek oversee the entirety of the K'uhad River's flow through the jungle, including both its northern and southern banks. Priests and mages, held in high esteem within the Mayek tribe, govern the city and make all major decisions. Among these leaders, a High Priest is selected to represent them in diplomatic engagements with other factions.

#### City defenses

Aghanat is crisscrossed with canals, each bridge fortified with watchtowers and sentries. Patrolling ships maintain vigilance over the waters. The city's defense includes the Zenari city guard and a contingent of mercenaries to ensure order.

#### Organizations

As a prime trading nexus, Aghanat attracts global factions and organizations, from the Leari Order and Greatwyrm Scribes to the Golden Hand, which integrates its soldiers into the city guard. Each organization has established a significant presence within the city.

#### Architecture

While Maanar-Kin boasts strength and grandeur in its architecture, Aghanat is celebrated as the zenith of Zenari aesthetic beauty. Its stunning stone buildings, temples dedicated to their gods, majestic bridges, and watchtowers commanding views of the canals, bustling docks, and a vast shipyard welcoming ships from every race and faction exemplify its splendor.

**City Canals**: These waterways span the entire city, linking the K'uhad River with the North Stjarna Sea. Bridges facilitate passage throughout Aghanat, with many guarded and some featuring watchtowers.

**City Hospital**: Home to the Sacred Blood, a healer organization predominantly active within Zenari territories but also found elsewhere. Supported by the High Priest, they have constructed a large hospital staffed by the finest Zenari healers capable of treating nearly any ailment.

**Avelia Harbor**: Named in honor of the water goddess Avelia, this harbor is one of the largest in the North Stjarna Sea. Open to all factions and organizations without strict trade restrictions, Aghanat ships patrol the docks to ensure utmost security.

#### Resources

Beyond the typical fishing and trading activities, Aghanat boasts developed industries in salt and spice production and active woodworking guilds, mainly focused on shipbuilding.

#### Lore

Since its foundation, Aghanat and its surrounding villages have faced constant threats from jungle beasts. Over time, the Zenari learned to coexist with these creatures, becoming their protectors and guardians. During the War of the Elements, Aghanat was a focal point of conflict. It saw the launch of its first fleet under Admiral Morrgataur and served as a base for Young Zaeor's northern campaign against the Human states. Following Ner'thazal's death at the siege of Solarion, when Amalgam forces drove the Zenari back, Aghanat was the first city they attacked. A coalition of Elves, Humans, Dragons, and the Dragonguard captured the city in less than a day. The Zenari quickly reclaimed Aghanat, later joining forces with Amalgam to combat the Demons collectively and seal the gates. Since then, leading Zenari architects have focused on the city's reconstruction, enhancing the canal system, and repairing war damages.
