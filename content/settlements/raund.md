Ra'und, home of the ancient Atash tribe, is a city in the barrens of Westglade known for its advanced technology, specifically the preservation of nature within glass domes.

#### Population

17,200 (60% Flame Zenari, 25% other Zenari, 15% other races)

#### Government

The Inis Protectorate governs with an authoritarian structure. The faction is led by a single individual, the Flame Zenari High Priest, also known as the Protector. This title is held for life, and a new Protector is selected from among the most influential and capable Zenari only upon the death of the incumbent.

#### City defenses

Ra'und is a fortified settlement with its own city guard, where the city head reports directly to the Protector. Additionally, smaller detachments of the Flame Zenari army routinely patrol the surrounding areas to ensure security.

#### Organizations

While Chandar may be the larger city and the capital of the Inis Protectorate, Ra'und is preferred for diplomatic meetings and relations due to its more favorable climate. The Circle of Gaia has a presence here, aiding in the maintenance of the glass domes. There is also periodic involvement from other global organizations like the Tajir Guild and the Raven Syndicate.

#### Architecture

Ra'und's architecture leverages the barren desert landscape, utilizing sandstone to give its multilevel temples a distinct and profound aesthetic.

**Glass Domes**: Ra'und’s advancements in agriculture and ecological preservation have led to the development of glass domes. These structures use arcana and natural materials to create diverse environments, supporting various plant and animal species.

**Sky Roost**: In addition to land connections and trade routes, Ra'und has constructed a Sky Roost for faster transportation. Trained Roc riders facilitate the swift movement of people and resources between cities.

**Forbidden Temple**: Built by the first priest of the Atash tribe, this temple in the western part of the city is now abandoned and surrounded by stone walls. Historical attempts to enter have resulted in no returns.

#### Resources

Thanks to its innovative glass domes, Ra'und is largely self-sufficient. The city also boasts numerous shops producing linen, spices, and magical items.

#### Lore

Originally, the lands of Ekresh were lush and resource-rich. Following the Zenari settlement, subsequent droughts and tribal wars over resources devastated the region, now known as Ekresh Desolace. The Atash tribe, traditionally focused on technology and arcana, has always been reluctant about the unification under the Protectorate, particularly resentful of having to report to a Protector typically from the Ixil tribe, which has historically prioritized war preparations.
