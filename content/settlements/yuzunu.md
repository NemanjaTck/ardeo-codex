Founded in 1340 BG, Yuzunu, the second city of the future Qi-Yian Trade League, was originally a small village settled by Mendalis shipbuilders, located between the Mendalis shipyard to the south and the Yama-Kani forest to the north. To this day, Yuzunu serves as a shipyard for Segmut and the formidable fleet of the Qi-Yian Trade League.

#### Population

17,100 (60% Qi-Yian human, 22% other human races, 18% other races)

#### Government

Merchant guilds control the city and dictate the laws. Every four years, a meeting is held among the major guilds of the Qi-Yian Trade League, during which three new chancellors are selected, one for each major city. The current chancellor of Yuzunu is Leena Desai.

#### City defenses

The guilds pay taxes for a mercenary City Guard known as the Whites, named after their pearl-colored armor. Commanded by a guild-elected general, the City Guard includes fighters from all around the world, making it common to encounter a Gnome or a Half-Giant among them. The Yuzunu Whites specialize in archery. Additionally, each guild maintains its own bodyguards and protection services.

#### Organizations

Yuzunu is not well-known among various factions and organizations, mainly because, despite having its own chancellor, the city’s politics are largely influenced by the more powerful Segmut. The Tajir guild has a presence in all cities of the Trade Guild, including their own offices in Yuzunu. The local organization, Yoshigo Woodsmen, comprises mages and hunters dedicated to the protection and reforestation of the Yama-Kani forest. Several shipbuilders' guilds are also active, working on various projects.

#### Architecture

The center of activity in Yuzunu is its impressive shipyard, currently undertaking several projects. Not only serving the Trade League, the shipyard also caters to smaller investors, guilds, and traders. Yuzunu's residents have a passion for wood and woodworking, evident in the wooden roofing, beautifully decorated parks, bustling markets, and wooden statues that adorn the city squares.

**Yuzunu Shipyard** - This vast shipyard covers the entire coastal part of Yuzunu, where there is always at least one Qi-Yian warship docked, with more patrolling the surrounding seas. The dock is divided into various sections, each designated for a specific purpose such as part construction, crew training, trading, and docking.

**Ameno Square** - The main city square, home to the chancellor's seat, featuring a stunning wooden statue of Ishhalor at its center.

**Dizamonten Quarter** - This military quarter was rapidly established after the War of the Elements to train the Whites, extend their reach, and ensure future border security.

#### Resources

The city’s main exports are wood and wooden products, including objects and weapons. Utilizing the nearby forest, the city harvests wood, berries, and engages in controlled hunting. The shipyard is the city's primary producer, known for its diverse activities.

#### Lore

Located in the northern territories of the Qi-Yian Trade League, Yuzunu has always faced threats from the militaristic Ragdar Kingdom to the north. However, a major war has never occurred. The borders have been redrawn multiple times, with many villages populated by Qi-Yian people falling under Ragdar rule and vice versa. The League's position as a trade partner and a resource exporter are likely the main reasons no Ragdar king has ever waged a campaign against it. During the War of the Elements, every able person was sent south to defend Segmut. After Segmut's fall, Yuzunu came under Zenari control within days. They established a presence in the city but did not populate it. Once Segmut was retaken, Zenari officials departed, leaving minimal impact on the city. However, this event led to the construction of the Dizamonten Quarter to improve future defenses.
