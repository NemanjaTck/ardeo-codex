Founded in 1111 BG, the Eye of Tahrid, the largest settlement of the Northern Dwarves, was established by exiles from the Ragdar Kingdom, led by Esteban and Illric. It now stands as the largest Dwarven shipyard and the home of the Stormson clan.

#### Population

22,800 (70% Dwarves, 15% other human races, 8% gnomes, 7% other races)

#### Government

The Eye of Tahrid, like other Dwarven cities and clans, does not have a ruler but is overseen by a caretaker in the role of a chieftain. Each family has one representative in the city's political structures. The city has a People's Council for daily and local activities, and a High Council for more significant matters like foreign relations and defenses. The current chieftain is Bragi Stormson, son of Borin Stormson.

#### City defenses

The Eye of Tahrid is protected by several small keeps that train and house the Stormson standing army. While the generals are often on important missions, lower-ranked officers command the city guards. The Stormson clan maintains a standing fleet and an admiral, with several warships always patrolling the canals leading to the Westglade ocean.

#### Organizations

The Tajir guild is present in both the Eye of Tahrid and Eirdar. Besides a few representatives of other global organizations, local guilds and organizations are gaining influence in the city. Embassies and representatives from Solarion, Nohenjo Daro, and Veles are housed within the city.

#### Architecture

The city is bordered by a forest to the east, a lake to the south, canals to the west, and icy wilderness to the north. Keeps safeguard the paths into the city, with the only walled part facing the eastern forest. Due to strong winds and winter conditions, wooden houses and buildings are rare. The Dwarves of the Stormson clan, being master stonemasons, heavily influence the architectural style.

**Immondar canals**: Multiple canals extend from the city to the western coast of the ocean, featuring shipyards, docks, and tall bridges as magnificent architectural feats beyond current advancements.
**Statue of Tahrid**: The city's central square is built around a colossal statue of Tahrid, the god of storms and destruction, crafted by the first settlers from a mix of iron and stone from the nearby Bargahai mountains. The statue stands over 60 feet tall, with city laws prohibiting any building from exceeding its height.

#### Resources

The largest mines on the western side of the Bargahai mountains are controlled by the Ragdar Kingdom and the city of Helburg. Disputes and skirmishes over direct ownership of the mines have frequently occurred between the Ragdar and the Northern Dwarves. There are also several smaller iron mines east of the Fundar forests, where Dwarves have established minor mining settlements to support the mines. The Fundar forests, particularly their northern parts, are treacherous, so the Dwarves are cautious when gathering wood for shipbuilding. The canals are abundant with fish, and the Dwarves take great pride not only in fishing but also in nurturing the growth of different species.

#### Lore

Even though Esteban and Illric endeavored to focus on establishing a new life in the north, the Eye of Tahrid's founding as a settlement by exiles from the Ragdar Kingdom left its inhabitants with a lasting connection to their original homeland. This connection spurred them to attempt to reclaim their lands on multiple occasions. The Dwarves launched three distinct campaigns against Ragdar, each under different leadership, with the initial two being somewhat more successful than the last. After their first campaign, which included an attempt to conquer Worrenheim—ending with it becoming a cursed ghost city—they never again approached any major settlements of the Ragdar Kingdom. In open field battles, the Dwarves were no match for the trained Ragdar forces. The second campaign concluded with the capture of a few border villages and two keeps, all of which were lost during the third and final campaign 50 years ago. Currently, both the Stormson clan and the Ragdar Kingdom have stationed forces along their borders, with tensions escalating on both sides.
