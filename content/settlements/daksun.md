No founding date is recorded for the city of Daksun, a location first discovered by Melandis scouts who ventured south. Daksun, inhabited by the mystical and friendly Shalari race, is now the second most significant city of the Qi-Yian Trade League, renowned for its otherworldly architecture and the distinct culture of the Shalari elves.

#### Population

15,000 (72% Shalari elves, 15% Qi-Yian humans, 8% Zenari, 5% other races)

#### Government

As part of the Trade League, Daksun elects its own chancellor, though the role is largely ceremonial. The Shalari do not have a single leader; instead, they follow a common set of rules, acting cohesively. Academics in Nohenjo Daro theorize that the Shalari could be a hive-mind species that traversed the Astral realm and settled on Ardeo long ago, though this has never been confirmed.

#### City defenses

Daksun is a well-fortified city, built to protect against the smaller tribes and wild animals of Westglade's southern regions. It boasts walls and towers for defense and maintains a standing army primarily for resolving local disputes. The city's crime rate is exceptionally low, and its inhabitants, as well as visiting tourists from other races, are simultaneously in awe and fear of the Shalari. Some believe that Shalari guards employ unseen methods of surveillance.

#### Organizations

Daksun does not officially host any global factions or organizations. While members of various groups are present, it is unusual to find a Shalari involved in such entities.

#### Architecture

Every structure, from the city gates to the harbor and the towers, captures the attention of visitors with its unique style and white marble construction. The city is built on a hill, featuring intricate streets, squares, and bridges that contribute to its otherworldly atmosphere.

**Shalari Lighthouse**: Situated atop the highest coastal hill, this functioning lighthouse serves as a beacon for Shalari swimmers and ships during nights and storms.

**Hall of Mirrors**: Located in the city's northern part, this tallest building serves as the residence for the city’s priests and mages. It is named for a grand chamber filled with magical mirrors that reflect not only one's appearance but also inner truths, past events, and potential futures.

**Daksun Harbor**: Known for its rocky coasts and challenging access, Daksun Harbor is a trading hub designed for the Shalari fleet's smaller, more maneuverable ships. The Shalari’s open-minded nature, reflected in their casual approach to relationships and marriages, makes the harbor's taverns popular spots.

#### Resources

The Shalari’s ability to breathe underwater facilitates activities like fishing and pearl diving, establishing them as major exporters of these resources. They have also developed methods to extract salt from sea water, becoming one of the largest salt exporters. While Daksun is known for its wine production, the city has never engaged in mass production; thus, their wine is mostly consumed locally. Nonetheless, their wine bottles are highly sought after globally, found in the collections of kings and emperors.

#### Lore

The history of Daksun prior to its discovery by Qi-Yian scouts in 1325 BG remains largely unknown. It was the last city to join the Trade League, 300 years after the initial contact with the then Melandis elves. The Shalari have always been welcoming towards other races and factions, yet they remain secretive about their heritage, laws, and history. During the War of the Elements, the Zenari forces never attempted to siege Daksun, deterred by its rocky terrain and formidable defenses. The Shalari did not partake in the defense of Segmut and Yuzunu; as part of their agreement upon joining the League, Shalari forces are bound never to leave Daksun.
