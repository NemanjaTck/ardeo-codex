Living in one of the oldest cities on the continent of Westglade, the Zenari who reside on the southwestern rocky coasts have quickly developed a deep understanding of the earth and its minerals. The City of Dogbor is built atop an immense crater in the ground and spans multiple levels, making it the largest city of the Rock Zenari and their people.

#### Population

15,500 (60% Rock Zenari, 30% other Zenari, 10% other races)

#### Government

The Rock Zenari are deeply traditional, valuing the old ways and respecting their elders. The Dulgum Dominion is led by a high priest, who is chosen every ten years by the city's elders and resides in Dogbor. Living under harsh natural conditions with scarce natural resources, the Rock Zenari hold a firm belief that the elders, having achieved old age in such demanding circumstances, are truly the most capable and wisest.

#### City defenses

Dogbor is a well-fortified city, constructed around and within a vast, seemingly endless pit. The city's garrison boasts a surprisingly large number of soldiers, with the main forces of the Dulgum Dominion stationed in nearby settlements. Dogbor features underground passages connecting it to two keeps, one to the north and one to the south, both manned by the city's garrison.

#### Organizations

Dogbor's proximity to a wealth of rock, mineral, and metal resources in its barren and rocky terrain makes it an attractive location for many, including the Tajir guild among others. Numerous local and global alchemist and blacksmithing guilds have established a presence in the city, along with smaller thieves' guilds, despite the heavy guarding of digging sites.

#### Architecture

The City of Dogbor is constructed on multiple levels within the endless pit. The ground level is partially open and fortified by walls, and partially integrated into the rock. The central forge is linked by bridges to the ground level, surrounded by various shops, artisans, and blacksmiths.

**Central Forge**: Situated at the city's heart, this is a hub for various industries, accessible from the ground level via stone bridges. Use of the forge requires monthly or yearly permits.

**Caravanserai**: Positioned on the ground level near the fortified city gates, it serves as a rendezvous point for traders and caravans. Here, one can purchase resources for the journey ahead, rent animals, or even hire a fully guarded caravan for transporting valuable goods.

**Dogbor Arena**: The Dulgum Dominion is renowned for its annual war games, a bloody, barehand wrestling competition attracting contestants worldwide. Although deaths are rare, they occur. Beneath the ground, a massive stone coliseum can accommodate over half the city's population for the games and military training.

#### Resources

Dogbor is encircled by various metal and mineral digging sites and two large quarries. With a lack of diverse natural resources and reliant on underground rivers for water, the city imports food, clothing, and other necessities while extensively exporting metals and stone.

#### Lore

The first Zenari conflicts began even before their migration from Mannar-Kin to the southern lands of Westglade. Conflict with the Atash tribe and depleted resources drove smaller factions of the Ixil tribe eastward to Dulgum Dominion lands, quickly leading to armed conflict. Over the years, Dulgum allowed Ixil settlers into its territory, eventually losing control and seeing some villages declare allegiance to the Ixil High Priest. Dulgum responded by reclaiming these villages and enacting a decree to halt further settlement. Weakened, Ixil withdrew from Dulgum lands.

A second conflict arose following the unification of two tribes and the creation ritual of the Flame Zenari. Perceiving a western threat, Dulgum acted swiftly but was overwhelmed by the newly established Inis Protectorate. The Flame Zenari then advanced on Dogbor, capturing it in less than a day. It was not until Jordeg extended his protection and initiated the ritual that would lead to the founding of Rock Zenari that Dogbor and Dulgum Dominion were restored.
