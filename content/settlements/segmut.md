Founded in 1376 BG, Mendalis is the first and oldest city of the Qi-Yian Trade League. It is one of the most significant trade hubs on the continent of Westglade and in the Westglade Ocean.

#### Population

30,000 (53% Qi-Yian human, 15% elves, 12% other human races, 8% halflings, 8% Zenari, 4% other races)

#### Government

The city is controlled and governed by merchant guilds. Every four years, a meeting among the major guilds of the Qi-Yian Trade League is held to select three new chancellors, one for each major city. The current chancellor of Segmut is Kai Madaia.

#### City defenses

The guilds fund the city’s defense by paying taxes for the mercenary City Guard, known as the Whites due to their pearl-colored armor. They are under the command of a general elected by the guilds. The City Guard is composed of fighters from across the world, making it common to encounter a Gnome or a Half-Giant among them. Each guild maintains its own bodyguards and protective measures independently.

#### Organizations

Segmut is home to various guilds, including merchants, artisans, masons, and carpenters. The Tajir guild, though globally recognized, is not among the city’s largest. Segmut, a major trade node and a leading supplier of pearls, attracts diverse organizations. Rumors suggest the presence of the Raven Syndicate, although unconfirmed. The Leari Order is also present, with members serving in the City Guard.

#### Architecture

The city is divided into districts and completely encircled by walls. Two docks, one for industrial use and the other for trade and tourism, have been developed. Traditionally focused on their sea fleet, the city recently ventured into skyship technology, acquiring two skyships and beginning construction of a skyport.

**The White Tower** - housing the chancellor's seat, is one of the largest towers in the world, located in the center of an artificial lake within the city walls. The city treasury, major guild offices, and the chancellor's residence are situated within the White Tower. The small island on which it stands is connected to the rest of the city by three bridges, symbolizing the three major cities of the trade league. The tower and lake were constructed post-War of the Elements.

**Main City Square** - The central location where different factions' embassies are situated.

#### Resources

As the largest trade hub on the western coast of Westglade, Segmut is a leading producer of jewelry and food, especially known for its advanced pearl and fish industries. The city’s taverns offer some of the rarest and most expensive fish worldwide, making Segmut a premier destination for luxury dining.

#### Lore

Since its establishment, Segmut (formerly Mendalis) quickly emerged as the principal trade center on the western coast. The elves, among the first settlers, established a unique language and culture, distancing themselves from their ancestors and other human groups. Known for their gold reserves and neutrality in regional conflicts, they maintained a delicate balance between the neighboring Zenari to one side and Ragdar to the north. During the War of the Elements, Mendalis was the initial target of the Storm Zenari due to its wealth. Unprepared for the Zenari onslaught, the city capitulated after a few months. The Zenari, seeing potential in the city, renamed it Vankor and initiated reconstruction efforts. As the war ended, the Zenari were compelled to withdraw, allowing the city's guilds to reclaim and rename it Segmut, which means "pearl of the sea." Although Zenari forces left, some Zenari people stayed, becoming a minority group within the city and leaving a lasting impact on its cultural and architectural landscape.
