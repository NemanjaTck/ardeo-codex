Veles, a city founded in 1389 BG, lies along the coast of the North Stjarna Sea. Its rapid development was spurred by fertile lands, abundant forests, numerous rivers, and a strategic location as a trade route between Westglade and the eastern continent of Veil Morass. Today, Veles is not only the largest city in northern Westglade but also one of the most populous cities in the world.

#### Population

35,000 (53% Moravia human, 18% elves, 15% other human races, 14% other races)

#### Government

The Tsardom of Moravia was established in 1012 BG by Bogoslav Meronevich, who set the Meronevich dynasty to rule for centuries. The current Tsar, Iliya Meronevich, resides in Veles, the capital of Knyazhevina Veles. The city also houses the Circle of Elders, the original leaders of the Moravian people, who now serve as advisors to the Tsar. This council comprises the oldest and wisest members from the prominent families of Moravia.

#### City defenses

Veles is protected by the Greenblade Battalion, a standing army present in both of the capital cities. The battalion includes high-ranking officers, druids, shapeshifters, and over a hundred soldiers within the city. The Tsar's personal guard, composed of noble Vlastela, addresses local issues and maintains peace. The elite Stag Knights patrol the city's outskirts, safeguarding the forests and borders.

#### Organizations

The forests surrounding the city serve as a base for the Circle of Gaia. Veles, with its attractive features, bustling docks, and diverse population, draws influence from various major organizations.

#### Architecture

Moravian architecture, reflecting a deep respect for nature, integrates green spaces, trees, and plants within its designs.

**Trees of Odom**: The city's landscape is marked by a dense forest and three ancient trees over a thousand years old. The Trees of Odom, connected to magical leylines, are a significant source of arcane power for the city and are heavily guarded. Two of these trees guard the northern and southern city gates, while the third stands near the docks.

**Skyport**: The third Odom tree also serves as a skyport, one of the first in the world.

**Elder Forest**: This dense woodland within Veles is known for its parks and wildlife, housing the Circle of Elders.

**Alchemy Guild Hall**: Known for its distinguished alchemists, Veles hosts annual world reunions of famous alchemists and herbalists. The Alchemy Guild Hall, showcasing a beautiful blend of Elven and Moravian architecture, is located near the city center.

#### Resources

The city is surrounded by forests and small rivers. Some unique herbs and potions are found exclusively in Veles. Wood from the forests of Moravia is highly sought after for various uses, including weapons, furniture, and construction across the Westglade continent. The city's docks facilitate a thriving trade in diverse goods.

#### Lore

The history of Veles includes a quiet transition of power from the Circle of Elders to Bogoslav, who self-proclaimed himself Tsar of the new lands. Moravia was never at war with surrounding factions until the War of the Elements. The reigns of the Meronevich dynasty have generally been stable, though not without challenges. An exception was under Evgen the Third, whose heavy taxation and divisive policies led to a civil uprising, ending in his demise. During the War of the Elements, Moravian armies were the first to respond to the Elves' call for help, sending entire regiments of the Greenblade Battalion. Realizing the threat to all races if Solarion fell, Tsar Agor the Second Meronevich acted decisively, dispatching the formidable Greenblade Battalion and the revered Stag Knights, led by General Ladazar Nemov. The Stag Knights' legendary charge at the Battle of Hazimat Fields turned the tide of battle, enabling a critical evacuation of Elven women and children to the safety of Solarion's walls.
