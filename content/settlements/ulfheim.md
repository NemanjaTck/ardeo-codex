Magnificient city on the Eden river, one of the two capitals of the Ragdar kingdom, and the biggest trading hub in the northern parts of Westglade continent.

#### Population

32,600 (45% Ragdar Human, 20% other Humans, 15% Elves, 10% Halfings, 10% other races )

#### Government

The Ragdar Kingdom is governed by a diarchy, with two kings ruling jointly. Ulfheim serves as the seat for one of the Ragdar Kingdom's kings and also hosts an appointed High Marshal, a former military officer, who acts as the governor.

#### City defenses

One or two active bastions of the Ragdar army are always present in the city. Additionaly, there are forces of City Guard, mostly focusing on smaller issues and magic prohibition.

#### Organizations

Local faction of Cloaks serve as law keepers and king's counsel. There is a seat of Leari Order in the city, and rumors of a Raven Syndicate cell operating inside the city underground.

#### Architecture

City is constructed on the banks of the Eden river, with bridges connecting the city walls to the fields, fishing wharfs and farms.

**Underground Bazaar**: With an active underground, Ulfheim is one of the rare cities with active markets offering a variety of things that couldn't be found easily elsewhere. Given the city ban on magic, underground provides access to magic items and other banned stuff.

**Sky High Roost**: Opposition to magic and an abundance of resources, leads to absence of skyships and presence of variety of sky creatures. Ulfheim offers transport and travel to nearby cities with its own fleet of Gryphons and Ydris.

**Slave pits**: Going east towards the mountains, there are infamous slave pits, where fights are organized to this day.

#### Resources

Ulfheim is famous for its abundant nature resources and fertile plains, focusing mostly on agriculture and forestry. A number of trade routes, land and river, are connecting the city with the other powerful trade nodes around the continent and across the ocean.

#### Lore

Founded in 1347 BG, as a congregation of small villages on the banks of the river Eden, Ulfheim quickly grew into a city, becoming a trade hub for the region. After accepting the slavery in 1139 BG, massive trade routes were build, connecting Ulfheim with all other parts of the continent. Rich families, wielded their wealth as a symbol of authority. Once the resistance grew and managed to kill Gundohar Lamga and overthrow his family, Ulfheim managed to ban slavery and with it magic, becoming one of the first cities in the world with prohibition of magic.
