Nestled within the Bargahai mountains, Eldrekar is a city largely hidden from view, with only its city gates visible from the outside. Miners from both sides of the Bargahai mountains established it as a sanctuary to protect themselves from the ravages of war and the menacing creatures inhabiting the mountains. Entry into the city requires a special pass obtained from clan guards in a nearby settlement.

#### Population

17000 (70% Dwarves, 10% Humans, 10% Gnomes, 5% Halflings, 5% other races)

#### Government

Eldrekar, the sole city and home of the Eldrar clan, follows the Dwarven tradition of leadership by a caretaker rather than a ruler. Each family elects a representative to participate in the city's political structures, which include a People's Council for daily and local matters and a High Council for significant issues such as foreign relations and defense. The current chieftain is Eurin Eldrar.

#### City defenses

The Eldrar clan and city inhabitants seldom venture beyond the city gates. Eldrekar boasts robust fortifications, including watchtowers constructed within the city itself. The city guard, known as the Fire Guard, consists of nearly 500 well-trained and equipped fighters.

#### Organizations

Global factions and organizations are prohibited within the city to ensure security. However, some organizations have established their presence in neighboring settlements.

#### Architecture

The architecture of Eldrekar is dominated by stone, gold, and iron, resulting in a magnificent cityscape that includes vast arches, stairways, elevators, and stone watchtowers, all of which leave visitors in awe.
**Forbidden Areas**: In their excavations, the Dwarves of Eldrar encountered deep mountain regions inhabited by terrifying creatures and guarding unknown treasures. For the populace's safety, these areas have been sealed off and guarded by the Fire Guard.
**Fire Pits**: The city's grandest hall houses statues of the gods Inis and Koval, before which eternal flames burn in fire pits. An annual festival involves sacrificing animals to these deities.

#### Resources

Eldrekar is rich in various metals, with active mining operations within the city itself. However, it trades little of its harvested resources. Hunters and gatherers depart daily, sometimes spending days in mountain cabins before returning with provisions. The city also taps into underground springs for clean water.

#### Lore

The Eldrar clan has never waged war against another faction, prioritizing the safety and protection of their people above all. The Fire Guard only leaves the city to respond to raids on nearby settlements by the Bjornblod or to confront the northern beasts and creatures. Their hunters are exceptionally skilled in survival tactics.
