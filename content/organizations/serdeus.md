In the center of the Eurenis continent lies Foirhone, a city in the mountains, settled by exiles from both Nohenjo Daro and Altamere. Their leader Ajean Montaris stumbled upon a hole that would later on become one of the massive gold mines surrounding the city of Foirhone. He believed it was given by gods to him and his people. His devout faith evolved into fanaticism and eventually madness. He founded the order of Serdeus, declared city state Foirhone as theocracy, and gave the order full control of the city and surrounding mines.
Serdeus was imagined as an order of priests and clerics that should spread religion to non-believers. They believed that the purpose of every single creature on the planet should be to serve the Gods. Even before the war, they became notoriously famous around the continent, for their brutal ways of torturing and executing people. Orcs from the nearby tribes were captured and tortured day and night, creating units of Martyrs, even more brutal to their own kin than the priests were.
